WHEREAS, Medicare and Social Security are essential programs that provide financial and health security to millions of America's seniors, including over a million Minnesota residents; and WHEREAS, these programs have served as a foundation of economic stability for seniors, disabled individuals, and veterans for generations; and WHEREAS, recent proposals and legislative efforts at the federal level have raised concerns about potential cuts, privatization, or other measures that could undermine the viability of Medicare and Social Security; and WHEREAS, any reductions in benefits or limitations on eligibility would place an undue burden on Minnesota's seniors and most vulnerable residents, jeopardizing their ability to afford health care, prescription medications, and basic living expenses; and WHEREAS, nearly half of older Americans have no retirement savings and more than half of older adults rely on Social Security for a majority of their income; and WHEREAS, Social Security lifts more than 16 million older adults and almost one million children out of poverty every year; and WHEREAS, ensuring the solvency and effectiveness of these programs is not only a matter of economic security but also a reflection of our commitment to the dignity and well-being of our residents; and NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Minnesota State Senate condemns any federal legislative or executive actions that would reduce or eliminate Medicare or Social Security for Minnesota seniors. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Minnesota State Senate hereby calls upon the United States Congress to reject any proposals that would diminish the strength of these critical programs and instead advocate for policies that enhance their long-term sustainability. BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of State of the State of Minnesota is directed to prepare enrolled copies of this memorial and transmit it to the Speaker of the United States House of Representatives, the Minority Leader of the United States House of Representatives, the Majority Leader of the United States Senate, the Minority Leader of the United States Senate, and Minnesota's Senators and Representatives in Congress.
