No. 88, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on May 16, 2026 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2honoring Shongleng Yang. 1.3WHEREAS, Shongleng Yang was born in 1959, in Xiangkhuang Province, Laos, and endured 1.4the unimaginable hardships of war and displacement following the fall of Long Tieng in 1975, 1.5becoming a refugee in his own homeland; and 1.6WHEREAS, driven by resilience and the unyielding hope for a better future, Shongleng and 1.7his family fled political persecution under the new communist regime, embarking on a treacherous 1.8journey to Thailand in 1978 before seeking refuge and political asylum in the United States in 1980; 1.9and 1.10WHEREAS, Shongleng settled in Minnesota in 1986, bringing with him a spirit of 1.11perseverance and compassion that would soon transform his community. His journey from adversity 1.12to opportunity inspired those around him as he embraced the values of hard work, generosity, and 1.13unity. Through his leadership and unwavering commitment to progress, he became a cornerstone 1.14of the Hmong community's growth and success in Minnesota; and 1.15WHEREAS, he began his path as a chef at the Cheeping Chinese Restaurant, demonstrating 1.16humility and hard work. In 1995, he established the first Hmong-owned home health care business 1.17in Minnesota, which served Hmong, Lao, Vietnamese, and other ethnic communities with dignity 1.18and respect; and 1.19WHEREAS, in 2009, Shongleng and his business partner Paul Vang cofounded the Hmong 1.20Village Shopping Center, a vibrant cultural and economic hub in St. Paul. The shopping center 1.21brought economic empowerment to the Hmong community and came to represent its resilience and 1.22unity; and 1.23WHEREAS, Shongleng has devoted his life to community service and cultural preservation, 1.24holding leadership roles in the Hmong American New Year, Yang Wang Meng Association of 2.1Minnesota, United Yang Family Organization, Neighborhood Development Center, and Frogtown 2.2Action Alliance, always guided by compassion and a profound sense of duty; and 2.3WHEREAS, his legacy serves as a beacon of hope and inspiration to countless families in 2.4Minnesota and beyond, reflecting the very spirit of the American dream; and 2.5NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it 2.6recognizes and honors Shongleng Yang for his lifetime of resilience, compassionate leadership, 2.7and triumphant contributions to the economic and cultural prosperity of the Hmong community 2.8and the state of Minnesota. 2.9BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Senate of the State of Minnesota extends its deepest 2.10gratitude to Shongleng Yang for his legacy of service, his commitment to community empowerment, 2.11and his inspiring journey of hope and triumph. 2.12BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare an 2.13enrolled copy of this resolution, to be authenticated by the Secretary's signature and that of the 2.14Chair of the Senate Rules and Administration Committee, and transmit it to Shongleng Yang as an 2.15enduring symbol of the Senate of the State of Minnesota's respect, admiration, and appreciation for 2.16his extraordinary contributions.
