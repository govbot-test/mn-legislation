BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin [121A.395] CERTIFIED SCHOOL CHAPLAINS. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Definitions. new text end new text begin As used in this section, "certified chaplain" means a chaplain certified by a national or state chaplaincy organization or ecclesiastically endorsed by a religious organization. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin School chaplain. new text end new text begin (a) A school district or charter school may employ or accept as a volunteer a certified chaplain to provide support, services, and programs for a student, staff member, or legal guardian of a student as authorized by the board of a school district or charter school. new text end new text begin (b) A certified chaplain employed by or volunteering at a school under this section is not required to be licensed by the Professional Educator Licensing and Standards Board. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Background studies. new text end new text begin The board of a school district or charter school must initiate a criminal history background study for each individual hired or volunteering under this section. The background study must include a review of information from the Bureau of Criminal Apprehension, including criminal history data as defined in section 13.87, and must meet all background check requirements under section 123B.03. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Prohibition. new text end new text begin A school district or charter school must not employ or accept as a volunteer under this section an individual who has been convicted of any offense that requires the person to register as a predatory offender under section 243.166, or a crime under a similar law of another state or the United States. new text end new text begin Subd. 5. new text end new text begin Cause of action. new text end new text begin A cause of action does not arise against a certified chaplain for an action taken or statement made while providing support, services, or programs for a student, staff member, or legal guardian of a student, unless the action or statement was maliciously, willfully, and deliberately intended to cause harm to, harass, or intimidate an individual seeking the support, services, or programs provided by the certified chaplain. new text end Sec. 2. new text begin APPROPRIATIONS;
