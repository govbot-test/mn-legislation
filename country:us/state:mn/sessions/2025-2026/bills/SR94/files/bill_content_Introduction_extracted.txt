No. 94, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on May 16, 2026 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2recognizing the conveners of From Mountains to Lakes: The Museums We Carry. 1.3WHEREAS, the Hmong people have carried their culture through storytelling, song, textiles, 1.4ceremony, and lived experiences long before arriving in Minnesota, holding knowledge in their elders as 1.5living museums; and 1.6WHEREAS, From Mountains to Lakes: The Museums We Carry was convened to bring Hmong 1.7elders, culture bearers, educators, and community leaders together to reflect on the importance of passing 1.8cultural knowledge to the next generation; and 1.9WHEREAS, the Minnesota Humanities Center plays a vital role in supporting communities at risk 1.10of cultural erasure and extinction by creating space, resources, and collaborative learning opportunities that 1.11protect cultural identity; and 1.12WHEREAS, this gathering was guided and hosted in partnership with the Shakopee Mdewakanton 1.13Sioux Community at the HoÄokata Ti Cultural Center, whose generosity in sharing their own journey to 1.14protect language, land, and culture offered powerful teachings and inspiration to Hmong communities and 1.15all Minnesotans present; and 1.16WHEREAS, participants reflected on how culture is sustained, not by preservation alone, but by 1.17practice, relationships, generosity, and the willingness to teach and learn from one another; and 1.18WHEREAS, Minnesota is home to one of the largest Hmong populations in the nation, and efforts 1.19to safeguard culture benefit not only the Hmong community but also the cultural richness and shared future 1.20of our state; and 1.21NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it recognizes 1.22and honors From Mountains to Lakes: The Museums We Carry for its leadership in cultural preservation, 1.23cross-cultural and intergenerational learning, and community unity. 1.24BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Minnesota Senate expresses gratitude to the Shakopee 1.25Mdewakanton Sioux Community for their generous sharing of culture, language, and teachings, and honors 1.26all Indigenous and Hmong culture bearers who carry the responsibility of cultural continuity. 2.1BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Minnesota Senate recognizes the Minnesota Humanities 2.2Center for its ongoing leadership in supporting communities working to protect culture, cultivate belonging, 2.3and sustain identity for future generations. 2.4BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare an enrolled 2.5copy of this resolution, authenticated by the Secretary's signature and that of the Chair of the Senate 2.6Environment, Climate, and Legacy Committee.
