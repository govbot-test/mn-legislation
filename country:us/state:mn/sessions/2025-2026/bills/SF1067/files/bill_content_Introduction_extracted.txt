BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin [299C.85] COLD CASE HOMICIDES; REVIEWS; REINVESTIGATIONS. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Definitions. new text end new text begin As used in this section, the following terms have the meanings given: new text end new text begin (1) "agency" means a state or local law enforcement agency; new text end new text begin (2) "authorized person" means an immediate family member of a victim, including a parent, parent-in-law, grandparent, grandparent-in-law, sibling, spouse, child, or stepchild; new text end new text begin (3) "cold case homicide" means a homicide: new text end new text begin (i) committed more than three years prior to the date of an application for a case file review; new text end new text begin (ii) previously investigated by a state, local, or federal law enforcement agency; new text end new text begin (iii) for which all probative investigative leads have been exhausted; and new text end new text begin (iv) for which no likely perpetrator has been identified; and new text end new text begin (4) "victim" means a natural person who died as a result of a cold case homicide. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Initial review. new text end new text begin (a) The head of an agency shall review the case file regarding a cold case homicide within the agency's jurisdiction upon the receipt of a written application submitted by an authorized person to determine if a full reinvestigation of the case would result in either the identification of probative investigative leads or a likely perpetrator. The review must include: new text end new text begin (1) an analysis of what investigative steps or follow-up steps may have been missed in the initial investigation; new text end new text begin (2) an assessment of whether witnesses should be interviewed or reinterviewed; new text end new text begin (3) an examination of physical evidence to see if all appropriate forensic testing and analysis was performed in the first instance or if additional testing might produce information relevant to the investigation; and new text end new text begin (4) an update of the case file using the most current investigative standards as of the date of the review to the extent it would help develop probative leads. new text end new text begin (b) If the head of the agency determines that the case does not satisfy the criteria to be considered a cold case homicide, a review under paragraph (a) is not required and the head shall issue a written certification to the authorized person stating that a review is not necessary because all probative investigative leads have been exhausted or that a likely perpetrator will not be identified. new text end new text begin (c) A review under paragraph (a) may not be conducted by a person who previously investigated the homicide at issue. new text end new text begin (d) The agency shall provide in writing to the authorized person as soon as reasonably possible: new text end new text begin (1) confirmation of the agency's receipt of the application under paragraph (a); and new text end new text begin (2) notice of the authorized person's rights under this section. new text end new text begin (e) Only one case file review shall be undertaken at any one time with respect to the same cold case homicide victim. new text end new text begin (f) The agency shall conclude its case file review and reach a conclusion about whether or not a full reinvestigation under subdivision 4 is warranted within six months of receiving the application for review. new text end new text begin (g) The agency may extend the time limit under paragraph (f) once for a period of time not to exceed six months if the agency makes a finding that the number of case files to be reviewed make it impracticable to comply with the limit without unreasonably taking resources from other law enforcement activities. For cases for which the time limit in paragraph (f) is extended, the agency shall provide notice and an explanation of its reasoning to the authorized person who filed the written application for review. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Application. new text end new text begin Each agency shall develop a written application to be used for authorized persons to request a case file review under subdivision 2. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Full reinvestigation. new text end new text begin (a) The agency shall conduct a full reinvestigation of the cold case homicide at issue if the review of the case file required by subdivision 2 concludes that a full reinvestigation of the cold case homicide would result in probative investigative leads. new text end new text begin (b) A full reinvestigation must include analyzing all evidence regarding the cold case homicide at issue for the purpose of developing probative investigative leads or a likely perpetrator. new text end new text begin (c) A reinvestigation required under paragraph (a) may not be conducted by a person who previously investigated the homicide at issue. new text end new text begin (d) Only one full reinvestigation may be undertaken at any one time with respect to the same cold case homicide victim. new text end new text begin Subd. 5. new text end new text begin Consultation and updates. new text end new text begin (a) An agency conducting a case file review or full reinvestigation under this section shall consult with the authorized person who filed the written application for review and provide the person with periodic updates during the case file review and full reinvestigation. new text end new text begin (b) The agency shall meet with the authorized person and discuss the evidence to explain to the person its decision whether or not to engage in the full reinvestigation provided for under subdivision 4 at the conclusion of the case file review. new text end new text begin Subd. 6. new text end new text begin Subsequent reviews. new text end new text begin (a) If a case file review is conducted and a conclusion is reached not to conduct a full reinvestigation, no additional case file review is required to be undertaken with respect to that case for a period of five years, unless there is newly discovered, materially significant evidence. An agency may continue an investigation absent an authorized person's application. new text end new text begin (b) If a full reinvestigation of a cold case homicide is completed, and a suspect is not identified at its conclusion, no additional case file review or full reinvestigation need be undertaken with regard to that case for a period of five years beginning on the date of the conclusion of the reinvestigation, unless there is newly discovered, materially significant evidence. new text end new text begin Subd. 7. new text end new text begin Withholding information. new text end new text begin Nothing in this section requires an agency to provide information to an authorized person that would endanger the safety of any person, unreasonably impede an ongoing investigation, violate a court order, or violate legal obligations regarding privacy. new text end new text begin Subd. 8. new text end new text begin Multiple agencies. new text end new text begin In a case where more than one agency conducted the initial investigation of a cold case homicide, each agency shall coordinate their case file review or full reinvestigation so that there is only one joint case file review or full reinvestigation occurring at a time. new text end new text begin Subd. 9. new text end new text begin Applicability. new text end new text begin This section applies to cold case homicides occurring on or after January 1, 1970. new text end new text begin Subd. 10. new text end new text begin Annual report. new text end new text begin (a) By January 30 of each year, each agency shall submit to the Bureau of Criminal Apprehension in a manner determined by the bureau a report describing actions taken and results achieved under this section during the previous calendar year. At a minimum, the report must include: new text end new text begin (1) the number of written applications filed with the agency for case file reviews; new text end new text begin (2) the number of extensions granted, and an explanation of the reasons for the extension; new text end new text begin (3) the number of cases where the agency head determined a case file review was not warranted; new text end new text begin (4) the number of cases in which a case file review under subdivision 2 was conducted but where a full reinvestigation was determined to be unnecessary; new text end new text begin (5) the number of full reinvestigations initiated or closed; and new text end new text begin (6) information on the results of any full investigations conducted. new text end new text begin (b) By March 15 of each year, the superintendent of the Bureau of Criminal Apprehension shall publish on the bureau's website and report to the chairs and ranking minority members of the senate and house of representatives committees with jurisdiction over criminal justice policy a summary of the reports it received from agencies under paragraph (a). new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
