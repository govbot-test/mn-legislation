No. 65, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on February 26, 2026 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2commending Troy Heck for his 32 years of public service in Benton County. 1.3WHEREAS, those civic-minded individuals who work tirelessly in their communities perform 1.4an important service deserving of special legislative recognition; and 1.5WHEREAS, Troy Heck has worked in the Benton County Sheriff's Office for 32 years, 1.6including for the last 12 as sheriff; and 1.7WHEREAS, on January 12, Sheriff Heck announced that he would not seek re-election and 1.8would retire from law enforcement when his term expires in January 2027; and 1.9WHEREAS, originally from Bloomington, Minnesota, Troy graduated from Bloomington 1.10Kennedy High School and earned a degree in law enforcement from Normandale Community 1.11College; and 1.12WHEREAS, Troy joined the Benton County Sheriff's Office as a patrol deputy in 1994 and 1.13was promoted to detective investigator in 1999. He was first elected sheriff in 2014; and 1.14WHEREAS, Troy has also served on the Central Minnesota Drug Task Force and was a 1.15crisis negotiator with the Stearns-Benton Special Emergency Response Team. He was named the 1.16Rice American Legion Officer of the Year in 2004 and selected as the Minnesota Sheriffs Association 1.17Supervisor of the Year in 2013; and 1.18WHEREAS, Troy plans on spending more time with his grandchildren, pursuing hobbies, 1.19and taking up professional opportunities as they present themselves. He does not plan on working 1.20for another law enforcement agency as a licensed deputy but expects to maintain contact with law 1.21enforcement in a support role; and 1.22NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it 1.23extends its deepest gratitude to Troy Heck for his public service and wishes him well in retirement. 1.24BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare an 1.25enrolled copy of this resolution, to be authenticated by the Secretary's signature and that of the 1.26Chair of the Senate Rules and Administration Committee, and transmit it to Troy Heck.
