A bill for an act
 relating to health; guaranteeing that health care is available and affordable for
every Minnesotan; establishing the Minnesota Health Plan, Minnesota Health
Board, Minnesota Health Fund, Office of Health Quality and Planning, ombudsman
for patient advocacy, and auditor general for the Minnesota Health Plan; requesting
an Affordable Care Act 1332 waiver; authorizing rulemaking; making conforming
changes; requiring a report; appropriating money; amending Minnesota Statutes
2024, sections 13.3806, by adding a subdivision; 14.03, subdivisions 2, 3;
15A.0815, subdivision 2; proposing coding for new law as Minnesota Statutes,
chapter 62X.

BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA:

ARTICLE 1

MINNESOTA HEALTH PLAN

Section 1.

new text begin [62X.01] HEALTH PLAN REQUIREMENTS.
 new text end

new text begin In order to keep Minnesota residents healthy and provide the best quality of health care,
the Minnesota Health Plan must:
 new text end

new text begin (1) ensure all Minnesota residents are covered;
 new text end

new text begin (2) cover all necessary care, including medical, dental, vision and hearing, mental health,
chemical dependency treatment, prescription drugs, medical equipment and supplies,
long-term care, and home care;
 new text end

new text begin (3) allow patients to choose their providers;
 new text end

new text begin (4) reduce costs by negotiating fair prices and by cutting administrative bureaucracy,
not by restricting or denying care;
 new text end

new text begin (5) be affordable to all through premiums based on ability to pay and elimination of
co-pays;
 new text end

new text begin (6) focus on preventive care and early intervention to improve health;
 new text end

new text begin (7) ensure that there are enough health care providers to guarantee timely access to care;
 new text end

new text begin (8) continue Minnesota's leadership in medical education, research, and technology;
 new text end

new text begin (9) provide adequate and timely payments to providers; and
 new text end

new text begin (10) use a simple funding and payment system.
 new text end

Sec. 2.

new text begin [62X.02] MINNESOTA HEALTH PLAN GENERAL PROVISIONS.
 new text end

new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end

new text begin Short title. new text end

new text begin This chapter may be cited as the "Minnesota Health Plan."
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 2. new text end

new text begin Purpose. new text end

new text begin The Minnesota Health Plan shall provide all medically necessary
health care services for all Minnesota residents in a manner that meets the requirements in
section 62X.01.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 3. new text end

new text begin Definitions. new text end

new text begin As used in this chapter, the following terms have the meanings
provided:
 new text end

new text begin (a) "Board" means the Minnesota Health Board.
 new text end

new text begin (b) "Plan" means the Minnesota Health Plan.
 new text end

new text begin (c) "Fund" means the Minnesota Health Fund.
 new text end

new text begin (d) "Medically necessary" means services or supplies needed to promote health and to
prevent, diagnose, or treat a particular patient's medical condition that meet accepted
standards of medical practice within a provider's professional peer group and geographic
region.
 new text end

new text begin (e) "Institutional provider" means an inpatient hospital, nursing facility, rehabilitation
facility, and other health care facilities that provide overnight care.
 new text end

new text begin (f) "Noninstitutional provider" means individual providers, group practices, clinics,
outpatient surgical centers, imaging centers, and other health facilities that do not provide
overnight care.
 new text end

ARTICLE 2

ELIGIBILITY

Section 1.

new text begin [62X.03] ELIGIBILITY.
 new text end

new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end

new text begin Residency. new text end

new text begin All Minnesota residents are eligible for the Minnesota Health
Plan.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 2. new text end

new text begin Enrollment; identification. new text end

new text begin The Minnesota Health Board shall establish a
procedure to enroll residents and provide each with identification that may be used by health
care providers to confirm eligibility for services. The application for enrollment shall be no
more than two pages.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 3. new text end

new text begin Premium remittance. new text end

new text begin All Minnesota residents must pay the plan premiums
beginning on the date when the resident becomes eligible under the plan. Minnesota residents
are eligible for the plan even if they have not filled out the enrollment form.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 4. new text end

new text begin Residents temporarily out of state. new text end

new text begin (a) The Minnesota Health Plan shall
provide health care coverage to Minnesota residents who are temporarily out of the state
who intend to return and reside in Minnesota.
 new text end

new text begin (b) Coverage for emergency care obtained out of state shall be at prevailing local rates.
Coverage for nonemergency care obtained out of state, or routine care obtained out of state
by people living in border communities, shall be according to rates and conditions established
by the board.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 5. new text end

new text begin Visitors. new text end

new text begin Nonresidents visiting Minnesota shall be billed by the board for all
services received under the Minnesota Health Plan. The board may enter into
intergovernmental arrangements or contracts with other states and countries to provide
reciprocal coverage for temporary visitors.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 6. new text end

new text begin Nonresident employed in Minnesota. new text end

new text begin The board shall extend eligibility to
nonresidents employed in Minnesota under a premium schedule set by the board.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 7. new text end

new text begin Business outside of Minnesota employing Minnesota residents. new text end

new text begin The board
shall apply for a federal waiver to collect the employer contribution mandated by federal
law.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 8. new text end

new text begin Retiree benefits. new text end

new text begin All persons who are eligible for retiree medical benefits under
an employer-employee contract shall remain eligible for those benefits.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 9. new text end

new text begin Presumptive eligibility. new text end

new text begin (a) An individual is presumed eligible for coverage
under the Minnesota Health Plan if the individual arrives at a health facility unconscious,
comatose, or otherwise unable, because of the individual's physical or mental condition, to
document eligibility or to act on the individual's own behalf. If the patient is a minor, the
patient is presumed eligible, and the health facility shall provide care as if the patient were
eligible.
 new text end

new text begin (b) Any individual is presumed eligible when brought to a health facility.
 new text end

new text begin (c) Any individual involuntarily committed to an acute psychiatric facility or to a hospital
with psychiatric beds is presumed eligible.
 new text end

new text begin (d) All health facilities subject to state and federal provisions governing emergency
medical treatment must comply with those provisions.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 10. new text end

new text begin Data. new text end

new text begin Data collected because an individual applies for or is enrolled in the
Minnesota Health Plan are private data on individuals as defined in section 13.02, subdivision
12, but may be released to:
 new text end

new text begin (1) providers for purposes of confirming enrollment and processing payments for benefits;
 new text end

new text begin (2) the ombudsman for patient advocacy for purposes of performing duties under section
62X.12 or 62X.13; or
 new text end

new text begin (3) the auditor general for purposes of performing duties under section 62X.14.
 new text end

Sec. 2.

Minnesota Statutes 2024, section 13.3806, is amended by adding a subdivision to
read:


new text begin Subd. 1d. new text end

new text begin Minnesota Health Plan. new text end

new text begin Data on enrollees under the Minnesota Health Plan
are classified under sections 62X.03, subdivision 10, and 62X.13, subdivision 6.
 new text end

ARTICLE 3

BENEFITS

Section 1.

new text begin [62X.04] BENEFITS.
 new text end

new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end

new text begin General provisions. new text end

new text begin Any eligible individual may choose to receive
services under the Minnesota Health Plan from any participating provider.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 2. new text end

new text begin Covered benefits. new text end

new text begin Covered health care benefits in this chapter include all
medically necessary care subject to the limitations specified in subdivision 4. Covered health
care benefits for Minnesota Health Plan enrollees include:
 new text end

new text begin (1) inpatient and outpatient health facility services;
 new text end

new text begin (2) inpatient and outpatient professional health care provider services;
 new text end

new text begin (3) diagnostic imaging, laboratory services, and other diagnostic and evaluative services;
 new text end

new text begin (4) medical equipment, supplies, including prescribed dietary and nutritional therapies,
appliances, and assistive technology, including prosthetics, eyeglasses, and hearing aids,
their repair, technical support, and customization needed for individual use;
 new text end

new text begin (5) inpatient and outpatient rehabilitative care;
 new text end

new text begin (6) emergency care services;
 new text end

new text begin (7) emergency transportation;
 new text end

new text begin (8) necessary transportation for health care services for persons with disabilities or who
may qualify as low income;
 new text end

new text begin (9) child and adult immunizations and preventive care;
 new text end

new text begin (10) reproductive and sexual health care;
 new text end

new text begin (11) health and wellness education;
 new text end

new text begin (12) hospice care;
 new text end

new text begin (13) care in a skilled nursing facility;
 new text end

new text begin (14) home health care including health care provided in an assisted living facility;
 new text end

new text begin (15) mental health services;
 new text end

new text begin (16) substance abuse treatment;
 new text end

new text begin (17) dental care;
 new text end

new text begin (18) vision care;
 new text end

new text begin (19) hearing care;
 new text end

new text begin (20) prescription drugs and devices;
 new text end

new text begin (21) podiatric care;
 new text end

new text begin (22) chiropractic care;
 new text end

new text begin (23) acupuncture;
 new text end

new text begin (24) therapies which are shown by the National Institutes of Health National Center for
Complementary and Integrative Health to be safe and effective;
 new text end

new text begin (25) blood and blood products;
 new text end

new text begin (26) dialysis;
 new text end

new text begin (27) adult day care;
 new text end

new text begin (28) rehabilitative and habilitative services;
 new text end

new text begin (29) ancillary health care or social services previously covered by Minnesota's public
health programs;
 new text end

new text begin (30) case management and care coordination;
 new text end

new text begin (31) language interpretation and translation for health care services, including sign
language and Braille or other services needed for individuals with communication barriers;
and
 new text end

new text begin (32) those health care and long-term supportive services currently covered under
Minnesota Statutes 2016, chapter 256B, for persons on medical assistance, including home
and community-based waivered services under chapter 256B.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 3. new text end

new text begin Benefit expansion. new text end

new text begin The Minnesota Health Board may expand health care
benefits beyond the minimum benefits described in this section when expansion meets the
intent of this chapter and when there are sufficient funds to cover the expansion.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 4. new text end

new text begin Cost-sharing for the room and board portion of long-term care. new text end

new text begin The
Minnesota Health Board shall develop income and asset qualifications based on medical
assistance standards for covered benefits under subdivision 2, clauses (12) and (13). All
health care services for long-term care in a skilled nursing facility or assisted living facility
are fully covered but, notwithstanding section 62X.20, subdivision 6, room and board costs
may be charged to patients who do not meet income and asset qualifications.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 5. new text end

new text begin Exclusions. new text end

new text begin The following health care services shall be excluded from coverage
by the Minnesota Health Plan:
 new text end

new text begin (1) health care services determined to have no medical benefit by the board;
 new text end

new text begin (2) treatments and procedures primarily for cosmetic purposes, unless required to correct
a functional or congenital impairment, restore or correct a part of the body that has been
altered as a result of injury, disease, or surgery, or determined to be medically necessary
by a qualified, licensed health care provider in the Minnesota Health Plan; and
 new text end

new text begin (3) services of a health care provider or facility that is not licensed or accredited by the
state, except for approved services provided to a Minnesota resident who is temporarily out
of the state.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 6. new text end

new text begin Prohibition. new text end

new text begin The Minnesota Health Plan shall not pay for drugs requiring a
prescription if the pharmaceutical companies directly market those drugs to consumers in
Minnesota.
 new text end

Sec. 2.

new text begin [62X.041] PATIENT CARE.
 new text end

new text begin (a) All patients shall have a primary care provider and have access to care coordination.
 new text end

new text begin (b) Referrals are not required for a patient to see a health care specialist. If a patient sees
a specialist and does not have a primary care provider, the Minnesota Health Plan may assist
with choosing a primary care provider.
 new text end

new text begin (c) The board may establish an online registry to assist patients in identifying appropriate
providers.
 new text end

ARTICLE 4

FUNDING

Section 1.

new text begin [62X.19] MINNESOTA HEALTH FUND.
 new text end

new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end

new text begin General provisions. new text end

new text begin (a) The Minnesota Health Fund, a revolving fund,
is established under the jurisdiction and control of the Minnesota Health Board to implement
the Minnesota Health Plan and to receive premiums and other sources of revenue. The fund
shall be administered by a director appointed by the Minnesota Health Board.
 new text end

new text begin (b) All money collected, received, and transferred according to this chapter shall be
