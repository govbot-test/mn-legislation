BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin APPROPRIATION; EQUITABLE COMMEMORATION OF ANNIVERSARY OF FALL OF SAIGON.new text end new text begin (a) $500,000 in fiscal year 2026 is appropriated from the arts and cultural heritage fund to the Board of Directors of the Minnesota Humanities Center to support programming commemorating the 50th anniversary of the fall of Saigon. Programming must recognize the cultural and historical contributions of Cambodian-, Lao-, and Vietnamese-Minnesotans. new text end new text begin (b) The appropriation under this section is intended to address historical funding inequities and underrepresentation of Cambodian-, Lao-, and Vietnamese-Minnesotan communities in previous commemorative initiatives. new text end new text begin (c) The Minnesota Humanities Center, in collaboration with the Council on Asian Pacific Minnesotans, must administer and implement an equitable process to allocate money directly to nonprofit organizations and initiatives representing Cambodian-, Lao-, and Vietnamese-Minnesotans. The process must incorporate input from and reflect the priorities of these communities and must ensure: new text end new text begin (1) a minimum allocation of money to each community to guarantee fair representation and participation; new text end new text begin (2) outreach to increase awareness of funding opportunities; and new text end new text begin (3) technical assistance and capacity-building support for nonprofit organizations with limited resources or experience accessing funding. new text end new text begin (d) The Minnesota Humanities Center, in collaboration with the Council on Asian Pacific Minnesotans, may establish methods of distribution that ensure equity and community-informed decision-making. Potential methods include but are not limited to: new text end new text begin (1) a grant-making process; new text end new text begin (2) direct allocations to nonprofit organizations for programming specific to their communities; and new text end new text begin (3) collaborative partnerships between the Minnesota Humanities Center and nonprofit organizations to codevelop and implement programming. new text end new text begin (e) Programming decisions must be determined by the respective Cambodian, Lao, and Vietnamese communities, ensuring alignment with their unique cultural and historical priorities. new text end new text begin (f) Of the amount appropriated under paragraph (a), the Board of Directors of the Minnesota Humanities Center may retain up to $....... to reimburse the center and other entities for costs incurred to provide technical assistance, capacity-building support, and other administrative support to nonprofit organizations seeking to access funding under this section. new text end new text begin (g) As part of the report required under Minnesota Statutes, section 138.911, subdivision 2, the Board of Directors of the Minnesota Humanities Center must outline: new text end new text begin (1) the allocation of money appropriated under this section, including a breakdown by community; new text end new text begin (2) a description of funded organizations and projects; new text end new text begin (3) the methods used to distribute money and ensure equity; and new text end new text begin (4) recommendations for future funding to support equitable programming for the Cambodian, Lao, and Vietnamese communities. new text end new text begin (h) Any money remaining after the initial programming is completed must be reallocated equitably among Cambodian, Lao, and Vietnamese communities for additional programming consistent with the purposes of this appropriation. This appropriation is available until expended. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
