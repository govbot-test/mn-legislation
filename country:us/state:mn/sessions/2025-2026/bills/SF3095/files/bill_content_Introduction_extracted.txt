BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin [237.083] BROADBAND FOR LOW-INCOME MINNESOTANS. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Definitions. new text end new text begin For the purposes of this section, the term "broadband service" has the meaning given in section 116J.39, subdivision 1, paragraph (b). new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Low-income service. new text end new text begin (a) new text end new text begin Every person, business, or corporation, acting directly or through an agent, that provides or seeks to provide broadband service in Minnesota must offer broadband service to low-income consumers when a household is eligible for or receiving: new text end new text begin (1) Minnesota family investment program benefits; new text end new text begin (2) Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program benefits; new text end new text begin (3) medical assistance; or new text end new text begin (4) the low-income home energy assistance program under United States Code, title 42, chapter 94, subchapter II, sections 8621 to 8629. new text end new text begin (b) The low-income broadband service under this section must provide a minimum download speed of 25 megabits per second. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Cost. new text end new text begin (a) Broadband service for low-income consumers under this section must be provided at a cost of no more than: new text end new text begin (1) $15 per month for service that provides a minimum download speed of 25 megabits per second; or new text end new text begin (2) $20 per month for service that provides a minimum download speed of 200 megabits per second. new text end new text begin (b) The prices under this subdivision must be inclusive of any recurring taxes and fees such as recurring rental fees for service provider equipment required to obtain broadband service and usage fees. Broadband service providers shall allow low-income broadband service subscribers to purchase standalone or bundled cable or phone services separately. new text end new text begin (c) After 30 days' notice to its customers and the commission, broadband service providers may increase the price of the service required by this section by the lesser of the most recent change in the consumer price index or two percent per year. Broadband service providers may make a price increase under this paragraph: new text end new text begin (1) once every five years for plans under paragraph (a), clause (1); and new text end new text begin (2) once every two years for plans under paragraph (a), clause (2). new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Exception. new text end new text begin The requirements in subdivisions 2 and 3 of this section do not apply to any broadband service provider providing service to no more than 20,000 households if the commission determines that compliance with the requirements would result in unreasonable or unsustainable financial impact on the broadband service provider. new text end new text begin Subd. 5. new text end new text begin Contracts. new text end new text begin Any contract or agreement for broadband service targeted to low-income consumers pursuant to this section or otherwise must have the same terms and conditions, other than price and speed, as the regularly priced offerings for similar service. new text end new text begin Subd. 6. new text end new text begin Promotion. new text end new text begin Every person, business, or corporation, acting directly or through an agent, that provides or seeks to provide broadband service in Minnesota shall make all commercially reasonable efforts to promote and advertise the availability of broadband service for low-income consumers, including but not limited to the prominent display of, and enrollment procedures for, the service on the provider's website and in any written and commercial promotional materials developed to inform consumers who may be eligible for service required by this section. new text end new text begin Subd. 7. new text end new text begin Reporting. new text end new text begin Every person, business, or corporation, acting directly or through an agent, that provides or seeks to provide broadband service in Minnesota shall submit to the commission no later than November 15 of each year a compliance report that includes: new text end new text begin (1) a description of the service offered pursuant to this section; new text end new text begin (2) the number of consumers enrolled in the service; new text end new text begin (3) a description of the procedures being used to verify the eligibility of customers receiving the service; new text end new text begin (4) a description and samples of the advertising or marketing efforts undertaken to advertise or promote the service; new text end new text begin (5) a description of all retail-rate products, including pricing, offered by the person, business, or corporation; new text end new text begin (6) a description, including speed and price, of all broadband products offered in Minnesota; new text end new text begin (7) the number of customers in arrears for the payment for broadband service, the percentage of customers in arrears that qualify for low-income broadband service, the number of households that have had their service terminated as a result of nonpayment, the number of customers whose service was terminated for arrears arising from nonpayment for services other than broadband service, and the number of households that have had their broadband service restored after being delinquent on their payments; and new text end new text begin (8) any other information the commission requires. new text end new text begin Subd. 8. new text end new text begin Commission duties. new text end new text begin By ..... and at least once every five years thereafter, the commission, in consultation with the Office of Broadband Development, shall determine if the minimum broadband download speed in this section should be increased to the Federal Communications Commission's benchmark broadband download speed or to another minimum broadband download speed if the Federal Communications Commission has not increased the benchmark. The commission shall also: new text end new text begin (1) inform the public about available broadband products, including retail-rate product offerings and low-income offerings; and new text end new text begin (2) periodically, but no less than once every five years, review eligibility and price requirements for the low-income service required pursuant to this section and inform the chairs and ranking minority members of the legislative committees with jurisdiction over utility regulation if the commission recommends changing the requirements to meet the needs of consumers. new text end new text begin Subd. 9. new text end new text begin Enforcement. new text end new text begin The attorney general shall enforce this section under section 8.31. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective .......... new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
