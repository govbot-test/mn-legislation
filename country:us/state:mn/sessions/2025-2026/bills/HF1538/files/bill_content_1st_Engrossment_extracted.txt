BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin APPROPRIATIONS; TEACHER APPRENTICESHIPS.new text end new text begin (a) $....... in fiscal year 2026 and $....... in fiscal year 2027 are appropriated from the workforce development fund to the commissioner of education for registered apprenticeships for teachers. Of these amounts, $....... each year is for a joint apprenticeship training committee to administer an apprenticeship program leading to teacher licensure. This is a onetime appropriation. new text end new text begin (b) To be eligible for funding under this section, a joint apprenticeship training committee must include the following members from throughout Minnesota: new text end new text begin (1) school districts or cooperative units under Minnesota Statutes, section 123A.24, subdivision 2; new text end new text begin (2) teacher preparation program providers approved by the Professional Educator Licensing and Standards Board; new text end new text begin (3) superintendents, principals, or other school administrators; new text end new text begin (4) licensed teachers; and new text end new text begin (5) exclusive representatives, as defined in Minnesota Statutes, section 179A.03, subdivision 8, of teachers. new text end new text begin (c) Funding must be used to develop or implement a registered apprenticeship program with the following five components: new text end new text begin (1) involvement by school districts or cooperative units to ensure apprentices develop the skills needed to be effective teachers; new text end new text begin (2) structured on-the-job training from an experienced mentor; new text end new text begin (3) related instruction from a teacher training program approved by the Professional Educator Licensing and Standards Board; new text end new text begin (4) increases in wages for participants as they gain higher skill levels; and new text end new text begin (5) a sequence of training and instruction resulting in a Tier 3 license issued by the Professional Educator Licensing and Standards Board. new text end new text begin (d) A joint apprenticeship training committee that receives funding under this section must: new text end new text begin (1) review guidelines for teacher apprenticeship programs developed by other Minnesota apprenticeship programs and other states with existing registered apprenticeship programs; and new text end new text begin (2) consider how apprenticeship programs may be used to fill identified shortages of licensed teachers. new text end new text begin (e) The commissioner of education must consult with the commissioner of labor and industry and the Professional Educator Licensing and Standards Board to: new text end new text begin (1) determine criteria for awarding funding under this section; new text end new text begin (2) establish oversight over the registered teacher apprenticeship programs; and new text end new text begin (3) develop recommendations for the legislature to improve the effectiveness of teacher apprenticeship programs as a pathway to teacher licensure. new text end new text begin (f) The commissioner of education must report to the legislative committees with jurisdiction over kindergarten through grade 12 education, higher education, and workforce development on how teacher apprenticeship program funding was used and recommendations for statutory or rule changes to facilitate improvement and expansion of teacher apprenticeship programs as a pathway to teacher licensure. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective July 1, 2025. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
