No. 89, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on May 16, 2026 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2honoring the life of Choua Thao. 1.3WHEREAS, Choua Thao was born on October 8, 1943, in Xieng Khouang Province, Laos, 1.4to a service-minded family known for its leadership. She was the daughter of Doua Kong and Qhua 1.5Vaag Thao; and 1.6WHEREAS, at 13 years old, Choua was recruited by International Volunteer Services and 1.7became the first Hmong woman to formally train as a nurse during the Secret War in Laos. She 1.8completed advanced training at the United States Udon Military Hospital in Thailand; and 1.9WHEREAS, Choua was a significant leader of her time. In a period marked by trauma, 1.10displacement, and conflict, she embodied service, courage, and unyielding leadership; and 1.11WHEREAS, in 1969, at just 22 years old, she became head administrator of Sam Thong 1.12Hospital, a CIA-run facility for frontline soldiers that averaged 500 patients per day. Her actions 1.13saved lives and strengthened the resilience of Hmong families and fighters alike; and 1.14WHEREAS, because of her close work with Americans, Choua's life was in danger when 1.15the communist regime took over the country. In May 1975, she fled Laos for Thailand, and 1.16immigrated to the United States on April 16, 1976; and 1.17WHEREAS, she first settled in Waupun, Wisconsin, and later moved to St. Paul, Minnesota, 1.18to complete her graduate studies at the University of Minnesota; and 1.19WHEREAS, for more than 40 years, Choua dedicated her life to serving refugee and 1.20immigrant communities in the United States. She served on the boards of numerous Hmong and 1.21Lao organizations in Minnesota; and 1.22WHEREAS, Choua retired in 2005 and later settled in the San Francisco Bay area, where 1.23she passed away peacefully on December 26, 2025, at the age of 82. She is survived by seven 1.24children, 16 grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren; and 2.1WHEREAS, Choua's legacy of sacrifice, leadership, and care continues to inspire Hmong 2.2Americans today and into the future; and 2.3NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it 2.4honors and recognizes Choua Thao for her enduring contributions to the Hmong community and 2.5the state of Minnesota. 2.6BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare an 2.7enrolled copy of this resolution, to be authenticated by the Secretary's signature and that of the 2.8Chair of the Senate Rules and Administration Committee, and transmit it to the family of Choua 2.9Thao.
