BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin [81B.01] SHORT TITLE. new text end new text begin This chapter may be cited as the "Solar Energy Systems Consumer Protection Act." new text end Sec. 2. new text begin [81B.02] SCOPE. new text end new text begin (a) This chapter applies to an agreement entered into on or after ..... This chapter does not apply to the transfer of title or rental of real property where a solar energy system is installed or expected to be installed. new text end new text begin (b) A solar energy system installed as a feature on a newly constructed residence is only subject to section 81B.06, subdivision 1. new text end new text begin (c) If an energy storage system is operated without being interconnected to a solar energy system or is interconnected to a solar energy system that was operating before the energy storage system was interconnected, this chapter applies only to the energy storage system. new text end Sec. 3. new text begin [81B.03] DEFINITIONS. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Application. new text end new text begin For purposes of this chapter, the following terms have the meanings given. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Agreement. new text end new text begin "Agreement" means a contract between a solar energy company and a consumer to: new text end new text begin (1) purchase or lease a solar energy system; or new text end new text begin (2) purchase solar energy under a power purchase agreement. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Commissioner. new text end new text begin "Commissioner" means the commissioner of commerce. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Consumer. new text end new text begin "Consumer" has the meaning given in section 325M.11, paragraph (g). new text end new text begin Subd. 5. new text end new text begin Energy storage system. new text end new text begin "Energy storage system" means a device that: new text end new text begin (1) stores electricity to be used at a later time; new text end new text begin (2) uses energy generated by a solar energy system or the electric grid to recharge; new text end new text begin (3) is located at a consumer's residence and connected to a consumer's solar energy system on the consumer's side of the electric meter; and new text end new text begin (4) provides stored electricity primarily to offset a consumer's consumption of electricity from the electric grid. new text end new text begin Subd. 6. new text end new text begin Fixture filing. new text end new text begin "Fixture filing" means a Uniform Commercial Code filing, recorded at the office at which the mortgage for a consumer's residence is filed, that establishes (1) a solar energy system is considered part of the consumer's real property, and (2) a lender has priority over other creditors if the consumer defaults on the loan, sells the residence where the solar energy system is installed, or declares bankruptcy. new text end new text begin Subd. 7. new text end new text begin Lease. new text end new text begin "Lease" has the meaning given to "consumer lease" under Code of Federal Regulations, title 12, section 1013.2, paragraph (e). new text end new text begin Subd. 8. new text end new text begin Lender. new text end new text begin "Lender" means a person or entity that makes a loan for a solar energy system. new text end new text begin Subd. 9. new text end new text begin License. new text end new text begin "License" means: new text end new text begin (1) a residential building contractor license, as defined in section 326B.802, subdivision 11; or new text end new text begin (2) a residential remodeler license, as defined in section 326B.802, subdivision 12. new text end new text begin Subd. 10. new text end new text begin Loan. new text end new text begin "Loan" means credit that is (1) offered or extended to a consumer in order to acquire a solar energy system, and (2) subject to the requirements of Code of Federal Regulations, title 16, section 433. new text end new text begin Subd. 11. new text end new text begin Person. new text end new text begin "Person" means a natural person, corporation, company, partnership, joint venture, or business entity. new text end new text begin Subd. 12. new text end new text begin Power purchase agreement. new text end new text begin "Power purchase agreement" means a contract under which a solar energy company (1) arranges for the design, permitting, financing, and installation of a solar energy system at a consumer's residence, and (2) sells the power generated to the consumer. new text end new text begin Subd. 13. new text end new text begin Registration. new text end new text begin "Registration" means registration of a solar energy company or solar energy salesperson with the commissioner under section 81B.05. new text end new text begin Subd. 14. new text end new text begin Solar energy company or company. new text end new text begin (a) "Solar energy company" or "company" means a person who engages in one or more of the following activities with respect to a solar energy system: new text end new text begin (1) offering or entering into a contract with a consumer to provide for a power purchase agreement, lease, or purchase; new text end new text begin (2) installation; new text end new text begin (3) project development, construction, or management; or new text end new text begin (4) providing operation and maintenance services. new text end new text begin (b) Solar energy company does not include: new text end new text begin (1) a natural person who self-installs a solar energy system on residential property owned by the natural person; or new text end new text begin (2) a third-party owner of a solar energy system or a lender for the purchase who does not sell or install solar energy systems. new text end new text begin Subd. 15. new text end new text begin Solar energy company fee. new text end new text begin "Solar energy company fee" means the amount paid or incurred by a solar energy salesperson or solar energy company to a lender for providing credit to a consumer. Solar energy company fee does not include an amount paid or incurred by a consumer to obtain financing, including a time-price differential or other finance charge, as defined under United States Code, title 15, section 1605. new text end new text begin Subd. 16. new text end new text begin Solar energy salesperson or salesperson. new text end new text begin "Solar energy salesperson" or "salesperson" means a person who is an employee or independent contractor of a solar energy company and who solicits, sells, negotiates, procures, or executes agreements for solar energy systems. new text end new text begin Subd. 17. new text end new text begin Solar energy system. new text end new text begin "Solar energy system" has the meaning given in section 216C.06, subdivision 17, and, if applicable, includes an on-site energy storage system interconnected with the solar energy system. A solar energy system must be installed at a natural person's residence. new text end new text begin Subd. 18. new text end new text begin Solicit. new text end new text begin "Solicit" means to contact a consumer to sell, lease, or install a solar energy system. new text end Sec. 4. new text begin [81B.04] REGISTRATION REQUIRED; SOLAR ENERGY COMPANIES AND SALESPERSONS. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Registration required. new text end new text begin A person is prohibited from engaging in the activities of a solar energy company or salesperson without first registering with the commissioner under this chapter. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Application and renewal. new text end new text begin When determining whether to issue or renew a registration, the commissioner must consider the application and renewal forms. As applicable, the application and renewal forms must include: new text end new text begin (1) the solar energy company's name, assumed names, doing business as names, including caller identification names, and business addresses; the name of each solar energy company owner and officer; and the name and primary work location of each solar energy salesperson; new text end new text begin (2) if a solar energy company is doing business under a name other than the solar energy company's legal name, documentation that the assumed name has been properly filed with the secretary of state or appropriate government office; new text end new text begin (3) the solar energy company's primary telephone number, email address, and website address used or intended to be used by the solar energy company and the company's solar energy salespersons to buy, sell, solicit, market, or deliver solar energy systems, energy storage systems, or a power purchase agreement; new text end new text begin (4) for the solar energy company and each solar energy company owner, officer, and salesperson, a disclosure of all criminal convictions by a court, including military courts, within the last ten years, except convictions for juvenile offenses and convictions relating to misdemeanor traffic citations and misdemeanor convictions involving driving under the influence, driving while intoxicated, driving without a license, reckless driving, or driving with a suspended or revoked license; new text end new text begin (5) a disclosure of civil judgments in favor of a government entity, and government entity orders entered, filed, or issued, against the solar energy company, the company's officers and owners, or the company's solar energy salespersons within the last ten years for violation of consumer protection or unfair trade practice laws; new text end new text begin (6) a disclosure of a settlement or other agreement with a government entity within the last ten years resolving concerns that the solar energy company, the company's officers and owners, or the company's solar energy salespersons violated consumer protection or unfair trade practice laws; new text end new text begin (7) a disclosure of any instance in which the solar energy company, the company's officers and owners, or the company's solar energy salespersons were at any time permanently or temporarily prohibited by a court of competent jurisdiction or ordered to cease and desist as the result of a government agency action from engaging in the actions of a solar energy company or salesperson; new text end new text begin (8) evidence of each salesperson's registration with Recheck, or an equivalent public registry identified by the commissioner; and new text end new text begin (9) copies of all advertising materials, marketing materials, agreements, and forms. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Notice of change in registration information. new text end new text begin A registered solar energy company must provide the commissioner written notice of a change in the solar energy company's name; assumed names; doing business as names; business addresses, including all business addresses at which the company or the company's solar energy salespersons conduct business; owners; email addresses; website domain names; or primary telephone number used by the company or the company's solar energy salespersons no later than 30 days after the date the change occurs. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Records. new text end new text begin A solar energy company must retain records of (1) all salespersons the company employs or contracts with, (2) all sales proposals provided to a consumer, (3) executed disclosures, and (4) stipulations upon which the terms of an agreement executed under section 81B.06 are based. Records under this subdivision must be retained for a period of not less than six years. new text end new text begin Subd. 5. new text end new text begin Registration fee. new text end new text begin (a) The fee for a registration under this chapter is: new text end new text begin (1) $....... for solar energy salespersons; and new text end new text begin (2) $....... for solar energy companies. new text end new text begin (b) The commissioner may adjust the registration fee on an annual basis as needed based on the cost to process registrations. new text end Sec. 5. new text begin [81B.05] REGISTRATION DENIAL, NONRENEWAL, REVOCATION, AND SUSPENSION. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Authority. new text end new text begin The commissioner may by order suspend, revoke, or refuse to issue or renew a solar energy company salesperson registration for: new text end new text begin (1) providing incorrect, false, misleading, or incomplete information to the commissioner or refusing to allow a reasonable inspection of (i) information and documents in the possession of the solar energy company, the company's salesperson, or a third party, or (ii) the company's, the company's salesperson's, or the third party's premises; new text end new text begin (2) obtaining or attempting to obtain a registration through misrepresentation or fraud; new text end new text begin (3) failing to meet any of the requirements of section 81B.06; new text end new text begin (4) making misrepresentations regarding government programs, utility relationships, or regulatory requirements; new text end new text begin (5) engaging in high-pressure sales tactics or preventing consumers from consulting with advisors; or new text end new text begin (6) violating this chapter or section 45.027, 325F.67, 325F.69, or 325F.694. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Solar energy company responsibility for actions of salespersons. new text end new text begin The commissioner may take action against a solar energy company for a violation of this chapter by the company's salespersons acting on behalf of or at the direction of the company. The commissioner may also take action against the salesperson. new text end Sec. 6. new text begin [81B.06] SOLAR ENERGY SYSTEMS CONSUMER PROTECTIONS. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Disclosure form; content; amendment. new text end new text begin (a) The commissioner must develop a written disclosure form that a solar energy company must provide to a consumer before an agreement is signed. The disclosure form must be in at least ten-point font and must contain, at a minimum: new text end new text begin (1) the name of the consumer; new text end new text begin (2) the name of the solar energy salesperson; new text end new text begin (3) the name, street address, telephone number, email address, and license or registration number of the solar energy company that is party to the agreement; new text end new text begin (4) the name, street address, telephone number, email address, and license or registration number of the company installing the solar energy system at a consumer's residence, if different from the solar energy company; new text end new text begin (5) a description of the project, including: (i) the capacity of the solar energy system in kilowatts-DC and, if applicable, the capacity of the energy storage system in kilowatt-hours; (ii) the brand and type of solar module, inverter, and monitoring device installed; and (iii) a statement that the solar energy system does not provide backup power without being interconnected with an energy storage system or other specified equipment that the consumer may decide to deploy; new text end new text begin (6) a solar energy system's technical specifications, including but not limited to: new text end new text begin (i) estimates of the first year's production, corrected for estimated production losses resulting from shade, snow, and soiling; and new text end new text begin (ii) the annual rate of decline in the amount of electricity generated by the solar energy system as a result of system degradation; new text end new text begin (7) a statement that the consumer continues to receive an electric utility bill and that the solar energy company or lender does not control the consumer's electricity rates; new text end new text begin (8) the estimated price of the solar energy system; new text end new text begin (9) an advisement suggesting that homeowners consult the homeowner's current mortgage loan servicer, originating lender, and insurance company regarding any potential impacts of installing a solar energy system; new text end new text begin (10) any performance or production guarantees or warranties made by the manufacturer or installer of the solar energy system and the transferability of the guarantees or warranties; new text end new text begin (11) information regarding how to file a complaint with the agency responsible for regulating the agreement, including: new text end new text begin (i) the name and contact information of the regulating agency; new text end new text begin (ii) a brief description of the agency's role in regulating solar energy system transactions; new text end new text begin (iii) a link to the agency's website or telephone number for filing complaints or requesting assistance; new text end new text begin (iv) information regarding how a consumer's personal data contained in an agreement is collected, used, stored, and shared; and new text end new text begin (v) information regarding a consumer's rights under sections 325M.10 to 325M.21 to understand and control the use of the consumer's personal data, including the ability to: new text end new text begin (A) access data collected about the consumer; new text end new text begin (B) request data corrections or deletions; new text end new text begin (C) opt out of data sharing or targeted advertising; and new text end new text begin (D) file a complaint with the appropriate agency if the consumer believes the consumer's rights have been violated; new text end new text begin (12) a description of requirements that must be met before an installed solar energy system is activated; new text end new text begin (13) a description of equipment a consumer may deploy in order to provide backup power to a solar energy system; new text end new text begin (14) assumptions and methodologies used to estimate postinstallation electric bill savings provided to the purchaser, including at a minimum estimated utility rates, rate increases, solar energy system production over time, and the rate of utility compensation for excess energy generated by the system at the time the agreement becomes effective; new text end new text begin (15) financial incentives from the utility, state, and federal government the consumer is eligible for and that have been incorporated into calculations of the total cost of the solar energy system made by the solar energy salesperson or solar energy company, including tax credits, and necessary steps a consumer must take in order to receive the incentives; new text end new text begin (16) a disclaimer stating that the solar energy company and solar energy salesperson are not tax advisors and are not qualified to give tax advice; new text end new text begin (17) the following statement: "THE SOLAR ENERGY SYSTEM DOES NOT PROVIDE ANY ELECTRICITY TO THE CUSTOMER DURING A POWER OUTAGE, and automatically disconnects from the utility grid in order to protect utility repair personnel from the risk of electric shock from electricity that could otherwise flow into the utility distribution system."; and new text end new text begin (18) the following statement: "A consumer may, subject to section 325G.08, withdraw from the proposed sale without penalty." new text end new text begin (b) A solar energy company or the installer of a solar energy system, immediately after determining that a material change in equipment, pricing, financing, production estimates, project timetables, or any other significant aspect of the proposed sale or operation of a solar energy system has occurred after the disclosure form has been provided to a consumer, must communicate the change to the consumer in writing and must provide the consumer an amended disclosure form incorporating the change. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Solar energy system purchase agreement; content. new text end new text begin An agreement must be in at least ten-point font and must contain, at a minimum: new text end new text begin (1) the information required in subdivision 1, clauses (1) to (8) and (10); new text end new text begin (2) the estimated date of completion of design and engineering of the solar energy system, and statements in close proximity that read: new text end new text begin (i) "The solar energy company must complete all work necessary to meet the timelines required by the Minnesota Distributed Energy Resource Interconnection Process."; and new text end new text begin (ii) "After receiving notice of all required permitting, interconnection, and other required approvals for installation of the solar energy system, the solar energy company must give the consumer written notice of the estimated installation start and completion dates."; new text end new text begin (3) an explanation of warranties and guarantees with respect to equipment or performance, including the transferability of the obligations; new text end new text begin (4) the total purchase price for the solar energy system, including all fees; new text end new text begin (5) if applicable, the payment schedule for the solar energy system; new text end new text begin (6) a description of the responsibilities of each party to maintain and repair the solar energy system; new text end new text begin (7) a disclosure of each party's responsibility to file the interconnection application with the consumer's utility and obtain necessary permits; new text end new text begin (8) if applicable, a description of a security interest filed against the solar energy system, including Uniform Commercial Code filings under chapter 336; and new text end new text begin (9) the documents that a seller for a home solicitation sale under section 325G.08 must provide to a consumer regarding a consumer's right to cancel the agreement within three business days after entering the agreement. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Solar energy system lease agreement; content. new text end new text begin In addition to the content required under subdivision 4, an agreement to lease a solar energy system must include, at a minimum: new text end new text begin (1) the total number and amount of payments required under the lease; new text end new text begin (2) the payment schedule for the leased system, including the number, amount, and due dates or periods of payments; new text end new text begin (3) the interest rate of the loan; new text end new text begin (4) a statement regarding the right of a consumer to purchase the solar energy system during or at the termination of the lease, including how the purchase price is calculated; new text end new text begin (5) a description of options available to the lender to transfer the lease to a third party and the conditions under which a transfer may be made; new text end new text begin (6) disclosure of fees received by the lender from the solar energy company; and new text end new text begin (7) disclosure of a fixed filing or other security interest a lender intends to file with respect to the solar energy system. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Solar energy system power purchase agreement; content of consumer agreement. new text end new text begin In addition to the content required under subdivision 3, a consumer's agreement with respect to a solar energy system power purchase agreement must include, at a minimum: new text end new text begin (1) the length of the power purchase agreement and the total number, amounts, and due dates of required payments; new text end new text begin (2) the monthly payment amounts during the first year of the power purchase agreement; new text end new text begin (3) the rate of payment increases and the date of the first increase, if applicable; new text end new text begin (4) the trigger event, amount, and timing of any onetime or recurring fees, including but not limited to system removal fees, Uniform Commercial Code notice removal and refiling fees, Internet connection fees, and automated clearinghouse fees; new text end new text begin (5) the manner in which a consumer receives invoices; new text end new text begin (6) a statement regarding the right of a consumer to purchase the solar energy system during or at the termination of the power purchase agreement, including how the purchase price is calculated; new text end new text begin (7) notice to a consumer that the owner of the system is filing a fixture filing on the solar energy system; and new text end new text begin (8) information regarding the transferability of the power purchase agreement if a consumer sells the residence where the solar energy system is installed. new text end new text begin Subd. 5. new text end new text begin Other provisions. new text end new text begin (a) A consumer is not required to make a payment on a loan or under a power purchase agreement before the consumer's utility has given the consumer permission to operate the solar energy system. new text end new text begin (b) A lender must withhold at least ten percent of the total amount owed to a solar energy company until the consumer's utility provides the consumer permission to operate the solar energy system. new text end new text begin (c) The sale of a solar energy system under this chapter is subject to section 325G.08 governing home solicitation sales. A solar energy company and a consumer have all the corresponding rights and responsibilities under section 325G.08 regarding a consumer's right to cancel an agreement without penalty. new text end new text begin (d) Subject to paragraph (b), a consumer has the right to cancel a sale subject to this chapter at any time without penalty. new text end new text begin (e) The solar energy company must ensure that a durable sticker or plaque containing the current contact information for all warranty and service work is permanently affixed in a conspicuous location adjacent to the main electrical service panel or the primary disconnect switch of the solar energy system. new text end new text begin (f) The provisions in this section must be included in the disclosure form and an agreement subject to this section. new text end new text begin Subd. 6. new text end new text begin Agreement review meeting. new text end new text begin (a) An agreement review meeting must be held between the consumer and the solar energy company or the company's designee at least ... days before an agreement is signed. During the meeting, the solar energy company or the solar energy company's designee must disclose the terms of the final agreement to the consumer, answer the consumer's questions, and obtain the consumer's affirmative acknowledgment and understanding of the agreement's terms. new text end new text begin (b) Notwithstanding any other law, both audio and video of the agreement review meeting must be recorded by the solar energy company or the company's designee, and must be made available to the consumer upon request. The solar energy company must maintain a copy of the recording for at least two years after the date the agreement review meeting occurs. new text end new text begin (c) The agreement review meeting must be conducted in the same language used in the disclosure form. new text end new text begin Subd. 7. new text end new text begin Enforcement. new text end new text begin (a) The commissioner must enforce this chapter. new text end new text begin (b) A solar energy company that contracts with a solar energy salesperson that violates this chapter is subject to the remedies under section 8.31 as if the solar energy company had committed the violation. This paragraph does not reduce or eliminate the liability of a solar energy salesperson who violates this section. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me? BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin [81B.01] SHORT TITLE. new text end new text begin This chapter may be cited as the "Solar Energy Systems Consumer Protection Act." new text end Sec. 2. new text begin [81B.02] SCOPE. new text end new text begin (a) This chapter applies to an agreement entered into on or after ..... This chapter does not apply to the transfer of title or rental of real property where a solar energy system is installed or expected to be installed. new text end new text begin (b) A solar energy system installed as a feature on a newly constructed residence is only subject to section 81B.06, subdivision 1. new text end new text begin (c) If an energy storage system is operated without being interconnected to a solar energy system or is interconnected to a solar energy system that was operating before the energy storage system was interconnected, this chapter applies only to the energy storage system. new text end Sec. 3. new text begin [81B.03] DEFINITIONS. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Application. new text end new text begin For purposes of this chapter, the following terms have the meanings given. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Agreement. new text end new text begin "Agreement" means a contract between a solar energy company and a consumer to: new text end new text begin (1) purchase or lease a solar energy system; or new text end new text begin (2) purchase solar energy under a power purchase agreement. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Commissioner. new text end new text begin "Commissioner" means the commissioner of commerce. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Consumer. new text end new text begin "Consumer" has the meaning given in section 325M.11, paragraph (g). new text end new text begin Subd. 5. new text end new text begin Energy storage system. new text end new text begin "Energy storage system" means a device that: new text end new text begin (1) stores electricity to be used at a later time; new text end new text begin (2) uses energy generated by a solar energy system or the electric grid to recharge; new text end new text begin (3) is located at a consumer's residence and connected to a consumer's solar energy system on the consumer's side of the electric meter; and new text end new text begin (4) provides stored electricity primarily to offset a consumer's consumption of electricity from the electric grid. new text end new text begin Subd. 6. new text end new text begin Fixture filing. new text end new text begin "Fixture filing" means a Uniform Commercial Code filing, recorded at the office at which the mortgage for a consumer's residence is filed, that establishes (1) a solar energy system is considered part of the consumer's real property, and (2) a lender has priority over other creditors if the consumer defaults on the loan, sells the residence where the solar energy system is installed, or declares bankruptcy. new text end new text begin Subd. 7. new text end new text begin Lease. new text end new text begin "Lease" has the meaning given to "consumer lease" under Code of Federal Regulations, title 12, section 1013.2, paragraph (e). new text end new text begin Subd. 8. new text end new text begin Lender. new text end new text begin "Lender" means a person or entity that makes a loan for a solar energy system. new text end new text begin Subd. 9. new text end new text begin License. new text end new text begin "License" means: new text end new text begin (1) a residential building contractor license, as defined in section 326B.802, subdivision 11; or new text end new text begin (2) a residential remodeler license, as defined in section 326B.802, subdivision 12. new text end new text begin Subd. 10. new text end new text begin Loan. new text end new text begin "Loan" means credit that is (1) offered or extended to a consumer in order to acquire a solar energy system, and (2) subject to the requirements of Code of Federal Regulations, title 16, section 433. new text end new text begin Subd. 11. new text end new text begin Person. new text end new text begin "Person" means a natural person, corporation, company, partnership, joint venture, or business entity. new text end new text begin Subd. 12. new text end new text begin Power purchase agreement. new text end new text begin "Power purchase agreement" means a contract under which a solar energy company (1) arranges for the design, permitting, financing, and installation of a solar energy system at a consumer's residence, and (2) sells the power generated to the consumer. new text end new text begin Subd. 13. new text end new text begin Registration. new text end new text begin "Registration" means registration of a solar energy company or solar energy salesperson with the commissioner under section 81B.05. new text end new text begin Subd. 14. new text end new text begin Solar energy company or company. new text end new text begin (a) "Solar energy company" or "company" means a person who engages in one or more of the following activities with respect to a solar energy system: new text end new text begin (1) offering or entering into a contract with a consumer to provide for a power purchase agreement, lease, or purchase; new text end new text begin (2) installation; new text end new text begin (3) project development, construction, or management; or new text end new text begin (4) providing operation and maintenance services. new text end new text begin (b) Solar energy company does not include: new text end new text begin (1) a natural person who self-installs a solar energy system on residential property owned by the natural person; or new text end new text begin (2) a third-party owner of a solar energy system or a lender for the purchase who does not sell or install solar energy systems. new text end new text begin Subd. 15. new text end new text begin Solar energy company fee. new text end new text begin "Solar energy company fee" means the amount paid or incurred by a solar energy salesperson or solar energy company to a lender for providing credit to a consumer. Solar energy company fee does not include an amount paid or incurred by a consumer to obtain financing, including a time-price differential or other finance charge, as defined under United States Code, title 15, section 1605. new text end new text begin Subd. 16. new text end new text begin Solar energy salesperson or salesperson. new text end new text begin "Solar energy salesperson" or "salesperson" means a person who is an employee or independent contractor of a solar energy company and who solicits, sells, negotiates, procures, or executes agreements for solar energy systems. new text end new text begin Subd. 17. new text end new text begin Solar energy system. new text end new text begin "Solar energy system" has the meaning given in section 216C.06, subdivision 17, and, if applicable, includes an on-site energy storage system interconnected with the solar energy system. A solar energy system must be installed at a natural person's residence. new text end new text begin Subd. 18. new text end new text begin Solicit. new text end new text begin "Solicit" means to contact a consumer to sell, lease, or install a solar energy system. new text end Sec. 4. new text begin [81B.04] REGISTRATION REQUIRED; SOLAR ENERGY COMPANIES AND SALESPERSONS. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Registration required. new text end new text begin A person is prohibited from engaging in the activities of a solar energy company or salesperson without first registering with the commissioner under this chapter. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Application and renewal. new text end new text begin When determining whether to issue or renew a registration, the commissioner must consider the application and renewal forms. As applicable, the application and renewal forms must include: new text end new text begin (1) the solar energy company's name, assumed names, doing business as names, including caller identification names, and business addresses; the name of each solar energy company owner and officer; and the name and primary work location of each solar energy salesperson; new text end new text begin (2) if a solar energy company is doing business under a name other than the solar energy company's legal name, documentation that the assumed name has been properly filed with the secretary of state or appropriate government office; new text end new text begin (3) the solar energy company's primary telephone number, email address, and website address used or intended to be used by the solar energy company and the company's solar energy salespersons to buy, sell, solicit, market, or deliver solar energy systems, energy storage systems, or a power purchase agreement; new text end new text begin (4) for the solar energy company and each solar energy company owner, officer, and salesperson, a disclosure of all criminal convictions by a court, including military courts, within the last ten years, except convictions for juvenile offenses and convictions relating to misdemeanor traffic citations and misdemeanor convictions involving driving under the influence, driving while intoxicated, driving without a license, reckless driving, or driving with a suspended or revoked license; new text end new text begin (5) a disclosure of civil judgments in favor of a government entity, and government entity orders entered, filed, or issued, against the solar energy company, the company's officers and owners, or the company's solar energy salespersons within the last ten years for violation of consumer protection or unfair trade practice laws; new text end new text begin (6) a disclosure of a settlement or other agreement with a government entity within the last ten years resolving concerns that the solar energy company, the company's officers and owners, or the company's solar energy salespersons violated consumer protection or unfair trade practice laws; new text end new text begin (7) a disclosure of any instance in which the solar energy company, the company's officers and owners, or the company's solar energy salespersons were at any time permanently or temporarily prohibited by a court of competent jurisdiction or ordered to cease and desist as the result of a government agency action from engaging in the actions of a solar energy company or salesperson; new text end new text begin (8) evidence of each salesperson's registration with Recheck, or an equivalent public registry identified by the commissioner; and new text end new text begin (9) copies of all advertising materials, marketing materials, agreements, and forms. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Notice of change in registration information. new text end new text begin A registered solar energy company must provide the commissioner written notice of a change in the solar energy company's name; assumed names; doing business as names; business addresses, including all business addresses at which the company or the company's solar energy salespersons conduct business; owners; email addresses; website domain names; or primary telephone number used by the company or the company's solar energy salespersons no later than 30 days after the date the change occurs. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Records. new text end new text begin A solar energy company must retain records of (1) all salespersons the company employs or contracts with, (2) all sales proposals provided to a consumer, (3) executed disclosures, and (4) stipulations upon which the terms of an agreement executed under section 81B.06 are based. Records under this subdivision must be retained for a period of not less than six years. new text end new text begin Subd. 5. new text end new text begin Registration fee. new text end new text begin (a) The fee for a registration under this chapter is: new text end new text begin (1) $....... for solar energy salespersons; and new text end new text begin (2) $....... for solar energy companies. new text end new text begin (b) The commissioner may adjust the registration fee on an annual basis as needed based on the cost to process registrations. new text end Sec. 5. new text begin [81B.05] REGISTRATION DENIAL, NONRENEWAL, REVOCATION, AND SUSPENSION. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Authority. new text end new text begin The commissioner may by order suspend, revoke, or refuse to issue or renew a solar energy company salesperson registration for: new text end new text begin (1) providing incorrect, false, misleading, or incomplete information to the commissioner or refusing to allow a reasonable inspection of (i) information and documents in the possession of the solar energy company, the company's salesperson, or a third party, or (ii) the company's, the company's salesperson's, or the third party's premises; new text end new text begin (2) obtaining or attempting to obtain a registration through misrepresentation or fraud; new text end new text begin (3) failing to meet any of the requirements of section 81B.06; new text end new text begin (4) making misrepresentations regarding government programs, utility relationships, or regulatory requirements; new text end new text begin (5) engaging in high-pressure sales tactics or preventing consumers from consulting with advisors; or new text end new text begin (6) violating this chapter or section 45.027, 325F.67, 325F.69, or 325F.694. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Solar energy company responsibility for actions of salespersons. new text end new text begin The commissioner may take action against a solar energy company for a violation of this chapter by the company's salespersons acting on behalf of or at the direction of the company. The commissioner may also take action against the salesperson. new text end Sec. 6. new text begin [81B.06] SOLAR ENERGY SYSTEMS CONSUMER PROTECTIONS. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Disclosure form; content; amendment. new text end new text begin (a) The commissioner must develop a written disclosure form that a solar energy company must provide to a consumer before an agreement is signed. The disclosure form must be in at least ten-point font and must contain, at a minimum: new text end new text begin (1) the name of the consumer; new text end new text begin (2) the name of the solar energy salesperson; new text end new text begin (3) the name, street address, telephone number, email address, and license or registration number of the solar energy company that is party to the agreement; new text end new text begin (4) the name, street address, telephone number, email address, and license or registration number of the company installing the solar energy system at a consumer's residence, if different from the solar energy company; new text end new text begin (5) a description of the project, including: (i) the capacity of the solar energy system in kilowatts-DC and, if applicable, the capacity of the energy storage system in kilowatt-hours; (ii) the brand and type of solar module, inverter, and monitoring device installed; and (iii) a statement that the solar energy system does not provide backup power without being interconnected with an energy storage system or other specified equipment that the consumer may decide to deploy; new text end new text begin (6) a solar energy system's technical specifications, including but not limited to: new text end new text begin (i) estimates of the first year's production, corrected for estimated production losses resulting from shade, snow, and soiling; and new text end new text begin (ii) the annual rate of decline in the amount of electricity generated by the solar energy system as a result of system degradation; new text end new text begin (7) a statement that the consumer continues to receive an electric utility bill and that the solar energy company or lender does not control the consumer's electricity rates; new text end new text begin (8) the estimated price of the solar energy system; new text end new text begin (9) an advisement suggesting that homeowners consult the homeowner's current mortgage loan servicer, originating lender, and insurance company regarding any potential impacts of installing a solar energy system; new text end new text begin (10) any performance or production guarantees or warranties made by the manufacturer or installer of the solar energy system and the transferability of the guarantees or warranties; new text end new text begin (11) information regarding how to file a complaint with the agency responsible for regulating the agreement, including: new text end new text begin (i) the name and contact information of the regulating agency; new text end new text begin (ii) a brief description of the agency's role in regulating solar energy system transactions; new text end new text begin (iii) a link to the agency's website or telephone number for filing complaints or requesting assistance; new text end new text begin (iv) information regarding how a consumer's personal data contained in an agreement is collected, used, stored, and shared; and new text end new text begin (v) information regarding a consumer's rights under sections 325M.10 to 325M.21 to understand and control the use of the consumer's personal data, including the ability to: new text end new text begin (A) access data collected about the consumer; new text end new text begin (B) request data corrections or deletions; new text end new text begin (C) opt out of data sharing or targeted advertising; and new text end new text begin (D) file a complaint with the appropriate agency if the consumer believes the consumer's rights have been violated; new text end new text begin (12) a description of requirements that must be met before an installed solar energy system is activated; new text end new text begin (13) a description of equipment a consumer may deploy in order to provide backup power to a solar energy system; new text end new text begin (14) assumptions and methodologies used to estimate postinstallation electric bill savings provided to the purchaser, including at a minimum estimated utility rates, rate increases, solar energy system production over time, and the rate of utility compensation for excess energy generated by the system at the time the agreement becomes effective; new text end new text begin (15) financial incentives from the utility, state, and federal government the consumer is eligible for and that have been incorporated into calculations of the total cost of the solar energy system made by the solar energy salesperson or solar energy company, including tax credits, and necessary steps a consumer must take in order to receive the incentives; new text end new text begin (16) a disclaimer stating that the solar energy company and solar energy salesperson are not tax advisors and are not qualified to give tax advice; new text end new text begin (17) the following statement: "THE SOLAR ENERGY SYSTEM DOES NOT PROVIDE ANY ELECTRICITY TO THE CUSTOMER DURING A POWER OUTAGE, and automatically disconnects from the utility grid in order to protect utility repair personnel from the risk of electric shock from electricity that could otherwise flow into the utility distribution system."; and new text end new text begin (18) the following statement: "A consumer may, subject to section 325G.08, withdraw from the proposed sale without penalty." new text end new text begin (b) A solar energy company or the installer of a solar energy system, immediately after determining that a material change in equipment, pricing, financing, production estimates, project timetables, or any other significant aspect of the proposed sale or operation of a solar energy system has occurred after the disclosure form has been provided to a consumer, must communicate the change to the consumer in writing and must provide the consumer an amended disclosure form incorporating the change. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Solar energy system purchase agreement; content. new text end new text begin An agreement must be in at least ten-point font and must contain, at a minimum: new text end new text begin (1) the information required in subdivision 1, clauses (1) to (8) and (10); new text end new text begin (2) the estimated date of completion of design and engineering of the solar energy system, and statements in close proximity that read: new text end new text begin (i) "The solar energy company must complete all work necessary to meet the timelines required by the Minnesota Distributed Energy Resource Interconnection Process."; and new text end new text begin (ii) "After receiving notice of all required permitting, interconnection, and other required approvals for installation of the solar energy system, the solar energy company must give the consumer written notice of the estimated installation start and completion dates."; new text end new text begin (3) an explanation of warranties and guarantees with respect to equipment or performance, including the transferability of the obligations; new text end new text begin (4) the total purchase price for the solar energy system, including all fees; new text end new text begin (5) if applicable, the payment schedule for the solar energy system; new text end new text begin (6) a description of the responsibilities of each party to maintain and repair the solar energy system; new text end new text begin (7) a disclosure of each party's responsibility to file the interconnection application with the consumer's utility and obtain necessary permits; new text end new text begin (8) if applicable, a description of a security interest filed against the solar energy system, including Uniform Commercial Code filings under chapter 336; and new text end new text begin (9) the documents that a seller for a home solicitation sale under section 325G.08 must provide to a consumer regarding a consumer's right to cancel the agreement within three business days after entering the agreement. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Solar energy system lease agreement; content. new text end new text begin In addition to the content required under subdivision 4, an agreement to lease a solar energy system must include, at a minimum: new text end new text begin (1) the total number and amount of payments required under the lease; new text end new text begin (2) the payment schedule for the leased system, including the number, amount, and due dates or periods of payments; new text end new text begin (3) the interest rate of the loan; new text end new text begin (4) a statement regarding the right of a consumer to purchase the solar energy system during or at the termination of the lease, including how the purchase price is calculated; new text end new text begin (5) a description of options available to the lender to transfer the lease to a third party and the conditions under which a transfer may be made; new text end new text begin (6) disclosure of fees received by the lender from the solar energy company; and new text end new text begin (7) disclosure of a fixed filing or other security interest a lender intends to file with respect to the solar energy system. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Solar energy system power purchase agreement; content of consumer agreement. new text end new text begin In addition to the content required under subdivision 3, a consumer's agreement with respect to a solar energy system power purchase agreement must include, at a minimum: new text end new text begin (1) the length of the power purchase agreement and the total number, amounts, and due dates of required payments; new text end new text begin (2) the monthly payment amounts during the first year of the power purchase agreement; new text end new text begin (3) the rate of payment increases and the date of the first increase, if applicable; new text end new text begin (4) the trigger event, amount, and timing of any onetime or recurring fees, including but not limited to system removal fees, Uniform Commercial Code notice removal and refiling fees, Internet connection fees, and automated clearinghouse fees; new text end new text begin (5) the manner in which a consumer receives invoices; new text end new text begin (6) a statement regarding the right of a consumer to purchase the solar energy system during or at the termination of the power purchase agreement, including how the purchase price is calculated; new text end new text begin (7) notice to a consumer that the owner of the system is filing a fixture filing on the solar energy system; and new text end new text begin (8) information regarding the transferability of the power purchase agreement if a consumer sells the residence where the solar energy system is installed. new text end new text begin Subd. 5. new text end new text begin Other provisions. new text end new text begin (a) A consumer is not required to make a payment on a loan or under a power purchase agreement before the consumer's utility has given the consumer permission to operate the solar energy system. new text end new text begin (b) A lender must withhold at least ten percent of the total amount owed to a solar energy company until the consumer's utility provides the consumer permission to operate the solar energy system. new text end new text begin (c) The sale of a solar energy system under this chapter is subject to section 325G.08 governing home solicitation sales. A solar energy company and a consumer have all the corresponding rights and responsibilities under section 325G.08 regarding a consumer's right to cancel an agreement without penalty. new text end new text begin (d) Subject to paragraph (b), a consumer has the right to cancel a sale subject to this chapter at any time without penalty. new text end new text begin (e) The solar energy company must ensure that a durable sticker or plaque containing the current contact information for all warranty and service work is permanently affixed in a conspicuous location adjacent to the main electrical service panel or the primary disconnect switch of the solar energy system. new text end new text begin (f) The provisions in this section must be included in the disclosure form and an agreement subject to this section. new text end new text begin Subd. 6. new text end new text begin Agreement review meeting. new text end new text begin (a) An agreement review meeting must be held between the consumer and the solar energy company or the company's designee at least ... days before an agreement is signed. During the meeting, the solar energy company or the solar energy company's designee must disclose the terms of the final agreement to the consumer, answer the consumer's questions, and obtain the consumer's affirmative acknowledgment and understanding of the agreement's terms. new text end new text begin (b) Notwithstanding any other law, both audio and video of the agreement review meeting must be recorded by the solar energy company or the company's designee, and must be made available to the consumer upon request. The solar energy company must maintain a copy of the recording for at least two years after the date the agreement review meeting occurs. new text end new text begin (c) The agreement review meeting must be conducted in the same language used in the disclosure form. new text end new text begin Subd. 7. new text end new text begin Enforcement. new text end new text begin (a) The commissioner must enforce this chapter. new text end new text begin (b) A solar energy company that contracts with a solar energy salesperson that violates this chapter is subject to the remedies under section 8.31 as if the solar energy company had committed the violation. This paragraph does not reduce or eliminate the liability of a solar energy salesperson who violates this section. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
