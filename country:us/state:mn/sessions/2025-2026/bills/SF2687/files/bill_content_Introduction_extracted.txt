BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin SAFE SCHOOL BUS SITE DEVELOPMENT WORK GROUP.new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Establishment; purpose. new text end new text begin By August 1, 2025, the commissioner of transportation must convene a safe school bus site work group to evaluate and identify strategies for the safe placement of school bus stop locations and make recommendations for any legislative changes necessary to implement the school bus stop standards. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Membership. new text end new text begin (a) At a minimum, the work group must consist of the following members: new text end new text begin (1) two members of the house of representatives, one appointed by the speaker of the house and one appointed by the minority leader of the house of representatives; new text end new text begin (2) two members of the senate, one appointed by the senate majority leader and one appointed by the senate minority leader; new text end new text begin (3) the commissioner of transportation, or a designee; new text end new text begin (4) the chief of the State Patrol, or a designee; new text end new text begin (5) the commissioner of education, or a designee; new text end new text begin (6) two members from each of the following school transportation groups, one of whom must be from the seven-county metropolitan area and one of whom must be from greater Minnesota: new text end new text begin (i) the Minnesota Association for Pupil Transportation; new text end new text begin (ii) the Minnesota School Bus Operators Association; and new text end new text begin (iii) the Minnesota Charter Bus Operators Association; new text end new text begin (7) one representative from each of the following agencies: new text end new text begin (i) the Office of Pupil Transportation Safety in the Department of Public Safety; new text end new text begin (ii) the Office of Special Transportation Services and Office of Freight and Commercial Vehicle Operations in the Department of Transportation; new text end new text begin (iii) the active transportation program in the Department of Transportation; and new text end new text begin (iv) the safe routes to school program in the Department of Transportation; new text end new text begin (8) two representatives from the statewide Toward Zero Deaths program; new text end new text begin (9) two representatives from the Minnesota Council on Disability, one of whom must have familiarity with the requirements of the Americans with Disabilities Act; new text end new text begin (10) a representative from a recognized child pedestrian safety organization; new text end new text begin (11) two representatives from the Minnesota School Boards Association, one of whom must be from the seven-county metropolitan area and one of whom must be from greater Minnesota; new text end new text begin (12) two superintendents of school districts in Minnesota, one of whom must be from the seven-county metropolitan area and one of whom must be from greater Minnesota; new text end new text begin (13) a representative from the Association of Metropolitan School Districts; new text end new text begin (14) one county engineer from the Minnesota County Engineers Association with experience in school bus stop design and the requirements of the Americans with Disabilities Act; new text end new text begin (15) one city engineer from the City Engineers Association of Minnesota with experience in school bus stop design and the requirements of the Americans with Disabilities Act; new text end new text begin (16) one representative from the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration, who serves as an ex-officio nonvoting member; and new text end new text begin (17) one representative from the National Center for Safe Routes to School or the Safe Routes National Partnership, who serves as an ex-officio nonvoting member. new text end new text begin (b) Appointments and designations to the work group authorized by this section must be completed by July 15, 2025. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Duties. new text end new text begin (a) The work group must analyze, study, develop, and evaluate standards for the safe siting of school bus stops in Minnesota. The analysis must: new text end new text begin (1) examine current state and local policies, regulations, and guidance related to school bus stop placement and safety and include a survey of other state policies or regulations related to school bus stop placement; new text end new text begin (2) analyze data on school transportation-related incidents, with a focus on incidents occurring at or near bus stops, and survey and collect data from school bus drivers on incidents where a student was endangered or put at risk at a school bus stop; new text end new text begin (3) analyze design factors and considerations used for the current placement of school bus stop sites for all road contexts where a school bus may stop; new text end new text begin (4) include best practices for the siting and selection of school bus stops, including consideration of factors such as: new text end new text begin (i) risk assessment and safety criteria, including but not limited to: new text end new text begin (A) a standardized risk assessment tool for evaluating potential and existing bus stops; new text end new text begin (B) identification and potential mitigations of high-risk environments, such as high-speed roads, blind curves, and industrial zones; and new text end new text begin (C) recommended minimum distances between bus stops, crosswalks, intersections, and driveways; new text end new text begin (ii) traffic flow and roadway conditions, including but not limited to: new text end new text begin (A) road contexts and types and their impact on bus stop safety; new text end new text begin (B) evaluation of historical crash data near potential or proposed bus stop locations; and new text end new text begin (C) road maintenance and improvement needs near bus stops; new text end new text begin (iii) pedestrian and student accessibility, including but not limited to: new text end new text begin (A) the availability of sidewalks, pedestrian crossings, and adequate lighting near bus stops; new text end new text begin (B) the selection of bus stops in rural or suburban areas where sidewalks are unavailable; new text end new text begin (C) the recommended maximum walking distance for students based on age or geography; and new text end new text begin (D) addressing special considerations for students with disabilities; new text end new text begin (iv) visibility and environmental conditions to allow approaching traffic adequate time to react to students or a stopped school bus, including but not limited to: new text end new text begin (A) an assessment of whether students are visible to drivers, including standards on sight distance to ensure a clear, straight-line distance within recommended federal guidelines; new text end new text begin (B) an evaluation of how weather conditions affect the safety of a bus stop; and new text end new text begin (C) recommendations on the use of reflective materials, signage, lighting, or other visibility or awareness methods; new text end new text begin (v) community engagement and parental input, including but not limited to: new text end new text begin (A) development of a framework for engaging parents, schools, and local governments in the bus stop selection process; and new text end new text begin (B) establishment of a feedback and reporting system for unsafe school bus stops to be easily reported and provide for community input; and new text end new text begin (vii) training and public awareness, including but not limited to: new text end new text begin (A) training for school district officials on how to assess and improve school bus stop safety; new text end new text begin (B) working with law enforcement and community organizations to promote safe driving behavior near bus stops; new text end new text begin (C) establishing public awareness initiatives about school bus stop arm laws and penalties for violating those laws; and new text end new text begin (D) identifying resources needed to provide training sessions and assist school districts in adopting and enforcing the work group's recommendations; and new text end new text begin (5) include feedback from local communities, including parents, students, and school personnel, on the proposed best practices formulated by the work group. new text end new text begin (b) The standards developed by the work group must be made in conjunction with local school districts, transportation authorities, and law enforcement agencies to gather input and ensure the feasibility of proposed best practices. new text end new text begin (c) The commissioner of transportation, in consultation with the commissioner of public safety and the State Patrol, must maintain publicly available information about the work group and provide an opportunity for public comment and feedback on the development of the report required under subdivision 5. new text end new text begin (d) Upon submission of the report required under subdivision 5, the commissioners of transportation, public safety, and education must develop guidelines for local school districts to implement the recommendations and best practices identified by the report. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Administration; grants. new text end new text begin (a) The work group must meet a minimum of 12 times. new text end new text begin (b) Member compensation and reimbursement for expenses are governed by Minnesota Statutes, section 15.059, subdivision 3. The commissioner of transportation must provide administrative support, meeting space, and staff support to the work group. new text end new text begin (c) The members of the work group must select a chair from its membership who must not be a commissioner or a commissioner's designee. new text end new text begin (d) The work group may accept grant funds from any federal, state, local, or nongovernmental source to support its work and offset any costs if accepting the money does not create a conflict of interest for the work group or its members. The Legislative Coordinating Commission may administer any grant money given to the work group. new text end new text begin Subd. 5. new text end new text begin Legislative report. new text end new text begin The work group must report its findings to the chairs and ranking minority members of the legislative committees with jurisdiction over transportation and education policy. The final report is due May 1, 2027, with an interim report due on March 1, 2027. new text end new text begin Subd. 6. new text end new text begin Expiration. new text end new text begin The work group expires on June 30, 2027. new text end Sec. 2. new text begin APPROPRIATION.new text end new text begin $....... in fiscal year 2026 is appropriated from the general fund to the commissioner of transportation for the safe school bus site development work group in section 1. This is a onetime appropriation and is available until June 30, 2027. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
