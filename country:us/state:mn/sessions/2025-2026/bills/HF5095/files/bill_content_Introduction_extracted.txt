BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin [124D.145] MINNESOTA IMAGINATION LIBRARY PROGRAM. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Establishment. new text end new text begin The Minnesota Imagination Library program is established under the Department of Education to provide age-appropriate books to children from birth to age five at no cost to families. The program shall be implemented through a partnership with the Dollywood Foundation to foster foundational literacy and support the goals of the Read Act under sections 120B.118 to 120B.124. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Definitions. new text end new text begin (a) For purposes of this section, the following terms have the meanings given. new text end new text begin (b) "Eligible child" means a child from birth to five years of age who is a resident of Minnesota. new text end new text begin (c) "Local affiliate" means a local 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization, school district, or public library that has entered into an agreement with the Dollywood Foundation to operate the program within a specific geographic area. new text end new text begin (d) "Statewide entity" means a Minnesota-based nonprofit organization selected by the commissioner to coordinate the program statewide. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Commissioner duties. new text end new text begin The commissioner of education must: new text end new text begin (1) select a statewide entity through a competitive process to coordinate the program; new text end new text begin (2) provide a state match of 50 percent of the cost of books and mailing for all children enrolled in the program through local affiliates; and new text end new text begin (3) align program outreach with the department's early literacy initiatives to ensure families are engaged in reading practices before kindergarten entry. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Statewide entity duties. new text end new text begin The selected statewide entity must: new text end new text begin (1) manage the daily operations of the program and serve as the primary liaison to the Dollywood Foundation; new text end new text begin (2) assist local affiliates with the 50 percent local match requirement through fundraising and grant-seeking efforts; new text end new text begin (3) develop and implement a statewide marketing plan to enroll at least 50 percent of eligible children by the end of fiscal year 2029; and new text end new text begin (4) coordinate with school districts to include Imagination Library enrollment information in early childhood screening appointments. new text end new text begin Subd. 5. new text end new text begin Local partner requirements. new text end new text begin To receive state matching funding, a local affiliate must: new text end new text begin (1) enter into an agreement with the Dollywood Foundation; new text end new text begin (2) provide 50 percent of the funding required for the cost of books and mailing for children in their service area; and new text end new text begin (3) manage the enrollment of children within the local affiliate's defined geographic boundaries. new text end new text begin Subd. 6. new text end new text begin Reporting. new text end new text begin By January 15 each year, the commissioner must submit a report to the legislative committees with jurisdiction over education finance and policy on the number of children enrolled per county, the number of books distributed, and an analysis of how the program has impacted kindergarten readiness data. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective the day following final enactment. new text end Sec. 2. new text begin APPROPRIATION.new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Department of Education. new text end new text begin The sum indicated in this section is appropriated from the general fund to the commissioner of education for the fiscal year designated. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Minnesota Imagination Library. new text end new text begin (a) For the Minnesota Imagination Library program under Minnesota Statutes, section 124D.145: new text end new text begin $ new text end new text begin ....... new text end new text begin ..... new text end new text begin 2027 new text end new text begin (b) This appropriation is available until June 30, 2029. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective the day following final enactment. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
