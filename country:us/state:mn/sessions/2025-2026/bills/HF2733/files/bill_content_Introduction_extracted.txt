BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin [604.145] LIABILITY; INJURY TO PETS. new text end new text begin (a) A person who injures another person's pet is liable to the owner of the pet for the cost of the injury, including medical bills, actual damages, emotional damages, and punitive damages as provided in sections 549.191 and 549.20. new text end new text begin (b) This section does not apply to: new text end new text begin (1) a licensed veterinarian who acts pursuant to a valid veterinary license and the acts would not be cause for revocation, suspension, or limitations according to section 156.081; new text end new text begin (2) a licensed veterinarian when the owner asks the veterinarian to cause the pet's death; new text end new text begin (3) a licensed veterinarian acting pursuant to state law or local ordinance; and new text end new text begin (4) any person authorized to kill a pet under state law. new text end new text begin (c) For the purposes of this section, "pet" means a nonhuman mammal, bird, or reptile impounded or held for breeding, or possessed by, cared for, or controlled by a person for the present or future enjoyment of that person or another, and does not include livestock. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
