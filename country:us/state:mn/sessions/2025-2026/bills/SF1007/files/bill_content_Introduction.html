11.13

A bill for an act
 relating to education; establishing education savings accounts; requiring rules;
proposing coding for new law in Minnesota Statutes, chapter 126C.

BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA:

Section 1.

new text begin [126C.75] EDUCATION SAVINGS ACCOUNTS FOR STUDENTS ACT.
 new text end

new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end

new text begin Title. new text end

new text begin This section may be cited as the "Education Savings Accounts for
Minnesota Students Act" (ESA-4-MSA).
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 2. new text end

new text begin Definitions. new text end

new text begin (a) For the purposes of this section, the following terms have the
meanings given.
 new text end

new text begin (b) "Commissioner" means the commissioner of the Department of Revenue.
 new text end

new text begin (c) "Curriculum" means programs and written plans for a particular content area or grade
level that provides students with learning experiences that lead to expected knowledge,
skills, and career and college readiness.
 new text end

new text begin (d) "Department" means the Department of Revenue or an organization with which the
commissioner contracts to implement any portion of this section.
 new text end

new text begin (e) "Education savings account" or "ESA" means the account to which funds are allocated
by the commissioner to the parent to pay for qualifying expenses to educate the ESA student
pursuant to the requirements of this section.
 new text end

new text begin (f) "Educational service provider" means an eligible school, tutor, or other person or
organization that provides education-related services and products to participating students.
 new text end

new text begin (g) "Eligible school" means:
 new text end

new text begin (1) a nonpublic school, including nonpublic online learning programs, where a student
can fulfill compulsory education requirements and that is recognized by the commissioner
or accredited by an accrediting agency recognized by the Minnesota Nonpublic Education
Council under section 123B.445, paragraph (a); or
 new text end

new text begin (2) a public high school, community college, or community and technical college that
offers one or more individual courses to eligible students in person or online.
 new text end

new text begin A child receiving instruction in a home school is eligible for an educational savings account
and may use it for eligible expenses, but the child's parent may not be reimbursed for the
time a parent spends providing instruction to the child.
 new text end

new text begin (h) "Eligible student" means any student who:
 new text end

new text begin (1) resides in Minnesota; and
 new text end

new text begin (2) is a member of a household whose total annual income does not exceed an amount
equal to four times the income standard used to qualify for reduced-price meals under the
National School Lunch Act, United States Code, title 42, section 1751, et seq.
 new text end

new text begin (i) "Eligible student participation maximum" means the maximum number of eligible
students receiving an ESA. In the first year, the eligible student participation maximum
shall be equal to five percent of the prior year's public school average daily membership.
For each subsequent year, the eligible student participation maximum shall increase by an
additional three percent of the prior year's statewide public school average daily membership.
 new text end

new text begin (j) "Eligible student selection priority" means the commissioner's acceptance of eligible
students in the ESA program based on the following order:
 new text end

new text begin (1) a student entering kindergarten;
 new text end

new text begin (2) a student who attended a public school, including a charter school, for the full school
year preceding anticipated participation in the ESA program;
 new text end

new text begin (3) a student whose sibling is participating in the ESA program; and
 new text end

new text begin (4) other eligible students.
 new text end

new text begin (k) "ESA program" means the program to implement education savings accounts.
 new text end

new text begin (l) "ESA student" means an eligible student who is participating in the ESA program.
 new text end

new text begin (m) "Parent" means a resident of Minnesota who is a parent, legal guardian, or other
person having legal custody of an eligible student under age 18. For an eligible student age
18 or over, "parent" means the eligible student unless a guardian or conservator has been
appointed, in which case it means the guardian or conservator.
 new text end

new text begin (n) "Postsecondary institution" means a college or university, including a career or
technical school, accredited by a state, regional, or national accrediting organization.
 new text end

new text begin (o) "Qualifying expense" means any expense used to educate an eligible student,
including:
 new text end

new text begin (1) tuition and fees at an eligible school;
 new text end

new text begin (2) payment to a tutor;
 new text end

new text begin (3) payment for purchase of curriculum, including any textbook and supplemental
materials required by the curriculum;
 new text end

new text begin (4) fees for transportation to and from an educational service provider paid to a
fee-for-service transportation provider;
 new text end

new text begin (5) tuition and fees for online learning programs or courses;
 new text end

new text begin (6) textbooks and fees for nationally standardized norm-referenced achievement tests
or for alternative assessments, including an assessment for a child with a disability as defined
in section 125A.02 or an English learner as defined in section 124D.59;
 new text end

new text begin (7) textbooks and fees for advanced placement examinations or similar courses and any
examination related to college or university admission;
 new text end

new text begin (8) educational services or therapies, including from paraprofessionals or educational
aides;
 new text end

new text begin (9) services provided by a public school, including extracurricular programs and
individual classes paid for as a tuition payment and not as an enrolled student in the public
school or as part of a shared time program under section 126C.19;
 new text end

new text begin (10) tuition, fees, and textbooks at a postsecondary institution;
 new text end

new text begin (11) no more than $400 in annual consumable school supplies, including school uniforms
necessary for the student's education;
 new text end

new text begin (12) computer hardware and software and other technological devices if an eligible
school, tutor, educational service provider, or licensed medical professional verifies in
writing that these items are necessary for the student to meet annual, measurable goals;
 new text end

new text begin (13) tuition and fees for summer education programs and after-school education programs,
but not after-school child care; or
 new text end

new text begin (14) any other expense approved by the commissioner.
 new text end

new text begin (p) "Tutor" means a person who:
 new text end

new text begin (1) is certified or licensed by a state, regional, or national certification or licensing
organization to teach;
 new text end

new text begin (2) has a valid teacher's license; or
 new text end

new text begin (3) has experience teaching at a postsecondary institution.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 3. new text end

new text begin Education savings account program. new text end

new text begin (a) An eligible student may participate
in the ESA program if the student's parent agrees:
 new text end

new text begin (1) to arrange for the provision of organized, appropriate educational services with
measurable goals to the participating student in at least the subjects of reading, writing,
mathematics, social studies, and science;
 new text end

new text begin (2) not to enroll the participating student in a public school, including a charter school,
for as long as the student participates in the program;
 new text end

new text begin (3) to use the funds deposited in a participating student's ESA only for qualifying expenses
to educate the student using any of the methods or combination of methods in this paragraph
that meet the requirement in clause (1); and
 new text end

new text begin (4) not to receive cash or cash-equivalent items, such as gift cards or store credit, from
refunds or rebates from a provider of services or products in the ESA program. Refunds or
rebates shall be credited directly to the participating student's ESA. Eligible schools,
postsecondary institutions, and educational service providers that serve participating students
shall provide parents with a receipt for all qualifying expenses.
 new text end

new text begin (b) A parent and a student may satisfy compulsory instruction requirements that the
student acquires knowledge and skills that are essential for the student's effective citizenship
and personal flourishing through the student's participation in the ESA program.
 new text end

new text begin (c) Payment for educational services through an ESA shall not preclude parents from
paying for educational services using non-ESA funds.
 new text end

new text begin (d) For purposes of continuity of educational attainment, a student who enrolls in the
ESA program remains eligible to receive monthly ESA payments until the participating
student enrolls in a public school, graduates from high school, or completes the school year
in which the student reaches the age of 21, whichever occurs first.
 new text end

new text begin (e) Any funds remaining in a student's ESA upon graduation from high school may be
used to pay for qualifying expenses at an accredited postsecondary institution.
 new text end

new text begin (f) Upon a participating student's graduation from a postsecondary institution, or after
any period of four consecutive years after graduation from high school during which the
student is not enrolled in a postsecondary institution, the participating student's ESA shall
be closed and any remaining funds shall be returned to the state general fund.
 new text end

new text begin (g) A participating student may enroll in the resident school district at any time after
enrolling in the ESA program, according to rules adopted by the commissioner providing
the least disruptive process for doing so. The parent must notify the commissioner that the
student has enrolled in the public school. Upon receiving notice of the enrollment, the
commissioner must close the student's ESA and return any remaining funds to the state
general fund.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 4. new text end

new text begin Students with disabilities. new text end

new text begin Participation in the ESA program by a student with
a disability is considered a parental placement under United States Code, title 20, section
1412, the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Participation in the program
does not affect the eligibility of a student with a disability for instruction and education
services under chapter 125A or otherwise affect the student's status under federal special
education laws.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 5. new text end

new text begin Commissioner's responsibilities. new text end

new text begin The commissioner is responsible for the
establishment and implementation of the ESA program. This may include adopting rules
and policies and contracting with a service provider to administer the ESA program and to
prevent ESA funds from being spent on nonqualifying expenses.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 6. new text end

new text begin Funding. new text end

new text begin (a) The commissioner must establish a funding amount for eligible
students in the ESA program that is equal to the state's adjusted per pupil formula allowance.
 new text end

new text begin (b) The commissioner may deduct up to a maximum of five percent annually in the first
two years of the ESA program and up to a maximum of three percent annually thereafter
from appropriations made to fund ESAs to cover the costs of overseeing and administering
the ESA program.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 7. new text end

new text begin Notices. new text end

new text begin (a) The commissioner must take reasonable actions to annually notify
all eligible students of:
 new text end

new text begin (1) the existence of the ESA program;
 new text end

new text begin (2) the amount of available funds per student;
 new text end

new text begin (3) the allowable expenses and the procedures to use the funds;
 new text end

new text begin (4) the program application procedures;
 new text end

new text begin (5) educational service providers;
 new text end

new text begin (6) the role of the commissioner and outside organizations with which the commissioner
contracts to administer the ESA program; and
 new text end

new text begin (7) other relevant information.
 new text end

new text begin (b) The commissioner must take reasonable actions to advise parents in low-income
families of their potential eligibility for the ESA program. The commissioner must take
reasonable actions to advise parents of students with disabilities about the rights of parentally
placed students under the IDEA and any applicable state laws and rules.
 new text end

new text begin (c) The commissioner must provide parents of participating students with a written
explanation of the allowable uses of ESAs, the responsibilities of parents, and the duties of
the commissioner. The commissioner must make the information available on the
department's website.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 8. new text end

new text begin Application process. new text end

new text begin (a) The commissioner must create a standard application
form that:
 new text end

new text begin (1) allows a parent to establish the student's eligibility and apply for an ESA;
 new text end

new text begin (2) requires a parent to agree to the terms and conditions of the ESA. The agreement is
effective upon the eligible student's acceptance into the ESA program; and
 new text end

new text begin (3) is readily available to interested families through various sources, including on the
department's website, and includes a copy of the procedural safeguards annually given to
parents.
 new text end

new text begin (b) The commissioner must establish reasonable periods of time of not less than 90 days
during which the commissioner must accept applications to enter the ESA program before
the start of the school year in the fall and not less than 60 days to enter into the ESA program
at the start of classes in the spring term.
 new text end

new text begin (c) The commissioner must begin accepting applications for the program for the fall
term of 2026 not later than December 1, 2025.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 9. new text end

new text begin Acceptance process. new text end

new text begin (a) The commissioner must establish procedures for
prioritizing and approving applications monthly consistent with the student participation
maximum in subdivision 2, paragraph (i), and the eligible student selection priority in
subdivision 2, paragraph (j).
 new text end

new text begin (b) Starting in the second year of the ESA program, the commissioner must automatically
renew annually the ESA student's participation in the ESA program unless funds are not
available, the ESA student graduates from high school, or the commissioner receives notice
that the ESA student has withdrawn from the program.
 new text end

new text begin (c) Starting in the second year of the ESA program and adjusted each year thereafter,
the commissioner may meet the eligible student participation maximum by granting
admittance to a greater number of applicants than the eligible student participation maximum.
The commissioner must base the additional acceptances on the ESA program's experience
of students who decline the acceptance.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 10. new text end

new text begin Student withdrawal from the ESA program. new text end

new text begin (a) Upon notice to the
commissioner that the ESA student has enrolled in a public school as a full-time student,
the commissioner must immediately stop depositing funds into the student's ESA. Funds
remaining in an ESA account that has been open and active for at least one full school year
remain available to be used for qualifying expenditures. The commissioner may close the
ESA when no funds remain in the student's ESA.
 new text end

new text begin (b) If an eligible student applies and is accepted into the ESA program after previously
withdrawing, payments into the student's existing ESA may resume if the ESA is still open
and active. A new ESA may be established if the commissioner closed the eligible student's
ESA.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 11. new text end

new text begin Contracting with service providers. new text end

new text begin To ensure the successful implementation
of the ESA program, the commissioner may contract with private organizations to administer
the ESA program. This authority includes contracting with a private firm to:
 new text end

new text begin (1) track and report to a parent a student's enrollment and completion of classes, grades,
test scores, and similar educational information;
 new text end

new text begin (2) perform the payment processing, manage financial functions, or provide financial
reporting to a parent about a student's ESA; and
 new text end

new text begin (3) provide information on a program-wide basis, redacted for information about
individual students.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 12. new text end

new text begin Payments. new text end

new text begin (a) The commissioner must make electronic payments to the ESAs
of participating students on a monthly basis unless there is evidence of misuse of the ESA
under this section.
 new text end

new text begin (b) Beginning with the 2026-2027 school year, the commissioner must issue ESA cards
to parents making expenditures under this section on behalf of a participating student. ESA
cards shall be issued to parents upon enrollment in the ESA program and shall expire when
the participating student's ESA is closed, except for the periodic expiration and replacement
of cards in the normal course of business. All unexpended amounts shall remain in the
student's ESA and be combined with the following year's allocation of ESA funds, subject
to subdivision 3, paragraphs (f) and (g).
 new text end

new text begin (c) The commissioner, taking into consideration requests from the parents of participating
students, must use merchant category classification (MCC) codes, or a similar system as
practicable and consistent with current technology, to identify categories of providers that
provide qualifying expenses. The commissioner must make a list of blocked and unblocked
MCC codes publicly available for purposes of the ESA program.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 13. new text end

new text begin Fraud prevention. new text end

new text begin (a) The commissioner must adopt a process for removing
educational service providers that defraud parents and for referring cases of fraud to law
enforcement.
 new text end

new text begin (b) The commissioner must establish or contract for the establishment of an online,
anonymous fraud reporting service and an anonymous telephone hotline for fraud reporting.
 new text end

new text begin (c) The commissioner may require an education service provider to post a surety bond
if the provider has operated for less than three years and is projected to receive more than
$100,000 annually from the ESA program.
 new text end

new text begin (d) The commissioner must notify the parent of any amount spent on nonqualifying
expenses within five business days by United States mail at the parent's home address. The
notification must explain the suspension, detail the violation, and request that the parent,
within 15 business days, either: (1) provide additional documentation justifying the
expenditure; or (2) repay the misspent amount. If the parent repays the amount within the
requested time frame, the offense must not be recorded and not be held in the parent's file.
If the parent does not provide sufficient documentation and refuses to repay the amount,
the commissioner must seek to recover the misspent funds using collections methods allowed
under state law. A student whose ESA has incurred three offenses within a consecutive
three-year period is disqualified from further participation in the ESA program.
 new text end

new text begin (e) If the commissioner determines that a parent has failed to comply with the terms of
the agreement as specified in subdivision 3, the commissioner must suspend the participating
student's ESA. The commissioner must notify the parent in writing within five business
days that the ESA has been suspended and that no further transactions will be allowed or
disbursements made. The notification must specify the reason for the suspension and state
that the parent has 21 business days to respond and take corrective action.
 new text end

new text begin (f) If the parent fails to respond to the commissioner, furnish reasonable and necessary
information, or make a report that may be required for reinstatement within 21 business
days, the commissioner may remove the participating student from the ESA program. A
decision of the commissioner under this subdivision is subject to judicial review under
sections 14.63 to 14.69. The decision of the commissioner is stayed pending an appeal.
 new text end

new text begin (g) The commissioner must refer cases of substantial misuse of funds to law enforcement
agencies for investigation if evidence of fraudulent intent and use of an ESA is obtained.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 14. new text end

new text begin Participation of education service providers. new text end

new text begin The commissioner must
establish an application process for an education service provider to request the
commissioner's approval to offer services under the ESA program. The application must
require the provider to describe the services it will provide and affirm the services are a
qualifying expense. The commissioner may investigate the application to determine whether
the provider is providing services allowed for as qualifying expenses. The commissioner
must approve an application to become an education service provider and provide services
that are qualifying expenses unless:
 new text end

new text begin (1) the provider notifies the commissioner of the withdrawal of its application or refusal
to receive payments from ESAs;
 new text end

new text begin (2) the commissioner determines that some or all of the provider's services are not
qualifying expenses; or
 new text end

new text begin (3) the provider or its management has been criminally convicted or found liable in a
civil case related to the provision of educational services, including theft, fraud, deceptive
trade practices, racketeering, or child abuse.
 new text end

new text begin Subd. 15. new text end

new text begin Scope. new text end

new text begin An eligible nonpublic school is autonomous and not an agent of the
state or federal government, and therefore:
 new text end

new text begin (1) the creation of the ESA program does not expand the regulatory authority of the
state, the commissioner, the department, any other government agency or officers, or any
school district to impose any additional regulation of nonpublic schools or educational
service providers beyond those necessary to enforce the requirements of the ESA program;
and
 new text end

new text begin (2) upon being recognized by the commissioner, an eligible school shall have the freedom
to provide for the educational needs of students and be able to offer diverse learning
opportunities. Upon such recognition, no additional mandates to participate in the ESA may
