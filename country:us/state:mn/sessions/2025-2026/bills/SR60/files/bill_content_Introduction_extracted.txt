No. 60, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on February 23, 2026 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2expressing the sense of the Senate condemning the Walz administration for inadequate 1.3oversight of government funds. 1.4WHEREAS, state agencies and state employees are entrusted to be good stewards of public 1.5funds; and 1.6WHEREAS, ensuring public programs have internal controls and safeguards is a basic 1.7protection against fraud, waste, and abuse of public; and 1.8WHEREAS, recent federal indictments, convictions, and investigations into widespread fraud 1.9within human services programs have exposed inadequate preventive measures across state 1.10government; and 1.11WHEREAS, former United States Attorney staff have publicly stated that as much as half 1.12of the $18 billion dollars paid out through several Medicaid programs could be fraudulent; and 1.13WHEREAS, bipartisan legislative efforts to improve oversight, prevent fraud, and create an 1.14independent Office of Inspector General met resistance from the Walz administration; and 1.15WHEREAS, Feeding Our Future engaged in the nation's largest COVID-era fraud scheme 1.16in Minnesota; and 1.17WHEREAS, the Feeding Our Future fraud scheme has led to the federal convictions of at 1.18least 45 people to date for their actions to steal $250 million and spend it on luxury cars, vacations, 1.19and expensive properties around the world; and 1.20WHEREAS, the Minnesota Department of Education was found to have failed to adequately 1.21investigate complaints regarding Feeding Our Future, allowing the fraud to continue year after 1.22year; NOW, THEREFORE, 2.1BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it condemns the inadequate 2.2actions the Walz administration has taken to prevent the defrauding of billions of public funds 2.3throughout various state programs.
