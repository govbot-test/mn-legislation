BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin [289A.15] INDIVIDUAL INCOME TAX RETURNS TO ALLOW ELECTION FOR ANATOMICAL GIFTS. new text end new text begin (a) Notwithstanding sections 270C.30 and 289A.08, subdivision 17, the individual income tax return must allow the taxpayer, and in the case of a joint return, the taxpayer's spouse, to indicate the taxpayer is willing to make an anatomical gift, as provided in this section. new text end new text begin (b) The individual income tax return and instructions must contain statements sufficient to comply with chapter 525A such that submission of the return to the commissioner makes the anatomical gift effective under that chapter for the taxpayer or the taxpayer's spouse. new text end new text begin (c) The Department of Revenue's website must include a link to the website of a federally designated organ procurement organization that describes: new text end new text begin (1) Minnesota laws regarding anatomical gifts; new text end new text begin (2) the need for and benefits of anatomical gifts; and new text end new text begin (3) the legal implications of making an anatomical gift, including the law governing revocation of anatomical gifts. new text end new text begin (d) For an individual who makes an election through the process required under this section, the commissioner must provide the name, date of birth, and address of the individual to the donor registry managed by the federally designated organ procurement organization. new text end new text begin (e) Data collected on tax returns or shared with a donor registry under this section is private data on individuals, as defined in section 13.02, subdivision 12. new text end new text begin (f) For a joint return, the return must allow each spouse to separately affirm that the spouse is willing to have their information transferred to the donor registry managed by the state's federally designated organ procurement organization. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective for returns for taxable years beginning after December 31, 2025. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
