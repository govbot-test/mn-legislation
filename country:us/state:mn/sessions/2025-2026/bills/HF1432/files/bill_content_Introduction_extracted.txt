BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin PILOT PROGRAM TO DEVELOP AND IMPLEMENT EXPERIENTIAL TRAINING REGIME FOR MANAGERIAL POSITIONS.new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Experiential training regime. new text end new text begin As used in this section, "experiential training regime" means a systematic manner of training managers to have a deep understanding of the work required in the positions over which they have managerial duties by training the managers to perform duties of those positions and to maintain knowledge of those positions through working shifts periodically on a schedule in those positions. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Obtain expert advice. new text end new text begin The commissioner of management and budget must contract for pilot programs with a private business consulting service with demonstrated expertise in improving customer service through developing and implementing an experiential training regime. Notwithstanding Minnesota Statutes, section 43A.047, the commissioner of management and budget is not required to demonstrate that existing staff are unavailable for this work before entering the contract with the private business consulting service. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Pilot programs. new text end new text begin (a) The commissioner of management and budget is required to institute pilot programs to develop and implement an experiential training regime for the following positions: new text end new text begin (1) managerial positions in the Department of Corrections with oversight for guards in correctional institutions; new text end new text begin (2) managerial positions in the Department of Human Services with oversight for employees who work in a group home setting; and new text end new text begin (3) managerial positions in the Department of Transportation with oversight for employees who work in road and bridge construction. new text end new text begin (b) The commissioners of corrections, human services, and transportation must cooperate with a consulting service hired by the commissioner of management and budget to conduct the pilot programs. new text end new text begin (c) The pilot programs must require the following work from the consulting service: new text end new text begin (1) develop an experiential training regime for the positions subject to the pilot program, including training materials and protocols; new text end new text begin (2) provide a plan for the department to implement the experiential training regime for the positions subject to the pilot program; new text end new text begin (3) identify other managerial positions in the agency where an experiential training regime for managers would likely benefit end users of services provided by the agency or would otherwise improve operations of the agency; new text end new text begin (4) submit to the commissioner of management and budget and to the chairs and ranking minority members of the legislative committees with jurisdiction over state government operations and reform a proposal for developing and implementing an experiential training regime for the additional positions identified in clause (3); and new text end new text begin (5) submit a report to the commissioner of management and budget and to the chairs and ranking minority members of the legislative committees with jurisdiction over state government operations and reform about the results of the pilot projects. new text end new text begin (d) Shifts worked by a manager to perform duties of the employees they supervise must not reduce the shifts worked by or available to be worked by employees. new text end new text begin (e) The pilot projects must be completed by July 1, 2026, and reports required under paragraph (c) must be submitted by August 1, 2026. The commissioners of corrections, human services, and transportation must institute the respective plans for experiential training regimes by January 1, 2027. new text end Sec. 2. new text begin APPROPRIATION.new text end new text begin $....... in fiscal year 2026 is appropriated from the general fund to the commissioner of management and budget to enter the contract authorized in section 1. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
