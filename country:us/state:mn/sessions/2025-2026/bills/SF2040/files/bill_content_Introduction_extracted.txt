BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin APPROPRIATION; SMALL MODULAR REACTOR VIABILITY REPORT.new text end new text begin (a) $500,000 in fiscal year 2026 is appropriated from the general fund to the commissioner of the Pollution Control Agency for a report on the viability and suitability of electricity supplied by small modular nuclear reactors in this state. By January 15, 2026, the commissioner, in consultation with the Environmental Quality Board, the Public Utilities Commission, the commissioner of natural resources, the commissioner of health, and the Critical Materials Recovery Advisory Task Force, must submit the report to the chairs and ranking minority members of the legislative committees with jurisdiction over energy finance and policy, environment and natural resources finance and policy, health finance and policy, and capital investment. The report must include: new text end new text begin (1) recommendations for enhanced environmental monitoring and protection, including recommendations related to: new text end new text begin (i) water quality monitoring and standards that exceed federal requirements; new text end new text begin (ii) waste storage and management protocols that meet or exceed current federal and state requirements; new text end new text begin (iii) water appropriation, consumption, and recycling requirements; new text end new text begin (iv) environmental justice considerations; new text end new text begin (v) integration with and enhancement of existing environmental regulations; and new text end new text begin (vi) comprehensive ecosystem protection measures; new text end new text begin (2) recommendations for infrastructure and operational requirements, including: new text end new text begin (i) grid integration requirements and associated costs; new text end new text begin (ii) necessary regulatory framework adaptations; new text end new text begin (iii) emergency response infrastructure needs; new text end new text begin (iv) public health monitoring systems and requirements; new text end new text begin (v) baseline health assessment protocols; new text end new text begin (vi) long-term health surveillance infrastructure; and new text end new text begin (vii) health care workforce development needs; new text end new text begin (3) an assessment of the economic viability of deploying small modular reactors in this state, including an assessment of: new text end new text begin (i) power purchase agreement structures, including public-private partnerships; new text end new text begin (ii) rate impacts across customer classes; new text end new text begin (iii) economic impacts on existing utilities and communities; new text end new text begin (iv) revenue generation potential and projections; and new text end new text begin (v) community benefit and ownership models; and new text end new text begin (4) options for community engagement and benefit structures, including: new text end new text begin (i) cooperative ownership models; new text end new text begin (ii) revenue-sharing mechanisms; new text end new text begin (iii) local business protection measures; and new text end new text begin (iv) rural development opportunities. new text end new text begin (b) The report required by this section must include recommendations for any legislation needed to implement the identified requirements and strategies.
