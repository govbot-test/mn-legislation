No. 93, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on May 16, 2026 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2honoring the courageous founders, leaders, and legacy of the Women's 1.3Association of Hmong and Lao, Inc. for advancing the rights, status, and 1.4empowerment of Hmong women and girls in Minnesota. 1.5WHEREAS, the state of Minnesota recognizes that the empowerment of women and girls 1.6is fundamental to a just and thriving society; and 1.7WHEREAS, Hmong women have long faced systemic barriers to equality, voice, and 1.8leadership, yet have demonstrated extraordinary resilience and strength in overcoming these 1.9obstacles; and 1.10WHEREAS, in the mid-1970's, as Hmong refugees resettled in Minnesota, a visionary group 1.11of Hmong women serving as interpreters, social workers, and community advocates saw the urgent 1.12need to uplift and protect Hmong women and girls; and 1.13WHEREAS, in 1978, these pioneering women, particularly Gaoly Yang, Choua Thao, Kia 1.14Moua Yang, and Kia Thao Ly, founded the Women's Association of Hmong and Lao, Inc. (WAHL), 1.15boldly challenging entrenched patriarchal norms, risking personal safety and reputation, and 1.16championing the fundamental rights and dignity of Hmong women; and 1.17WHEREAS, WAHL provided life-changing social services, leadership development, 1.18mentorship, and advocacy that empowered Hmong women and girls to define their own destinies, 1.19claim space in public life, and become agents of change; and 1.20WHEREAS, WAHL confronted the difficult issues of domestic violence and polygamy 1.21within the community, forging partnerships with law enforcement, shelters, and advocacy 1.22organizations to ensure the safety, freedom, and rights of Hmong women; and 1.23WHEREAS, WAHL created the first culturally specific programs for vulnerable and 1.24homebound Hmong elders, a service that continues to strengthen the community; and 1.25WHEREAS, over 24 years, WAHL has nurtured the leadership of more than 100 Hmong 1.26women, ignited a movement for Hmong women's rights in Minnesota and across the United States, 1.27and expanded opportunities for women to lead as organizers, advocates, and changemakers; and 1.28WHEREAS, the alumni and spirit of WAHL continue to inspire generations of women who 1.29carry forward its mission of equity, dignity, and justice by advancing deep cultural change and 1.30building a more inclusive Minnesota; and 1.31NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it 1.32honors and commends the courageous founders, board members, staff, and volunteers of the 1.33Women's Association of Hmong and Lao, Inc. for their historic and enduring contributions to the 1.34rights and empowerment of Hmong women and girls. 1.35BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Senate celebrates the lasting legacy of WAHL in 1.36transforming lives, uplifting voices, and forging a stronger, more equitable Minnesota for all.
