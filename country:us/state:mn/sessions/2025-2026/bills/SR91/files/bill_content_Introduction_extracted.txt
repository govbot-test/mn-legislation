No. 91, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on May 16, 2026 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2honoring the life and legacy of Za Xiong John Thao. 1.3WHEREAS, Za Xiong John Thao was born on August 25, 1951, in Houei Keui, Laos, to 1.4Cha Dang Thao and Xang Yang Thao. As the eldest of ten siblings, he embraced the weight of 1.5responsibility from an early age, serving as a protector, role model, and guide to his brothers and 1.6sisters; and 1.7WHEREAS, at just 14 years old, Za Xiong courageously volunteered to fight in the Secret 1.8War in Laos and was selected into the elite Thai-trained Special Operation Team. Despite his small 1.9stature, he completed grueling parachute and guerrilla warfare training in Hua Hin, Thailand, earning 1.10the admiration of his peers, trainers, and the Thai king himself; and 1.11WHEREAS, he served with valor from 1961 to 1966 in high-risk combat and intelligence 1.12missions across Laos, including assignments with Team Kick and Drop and later as a radar operator 1.13and logistics officer with Air America and the CIA under the code name "Penguin." On each mission, 1.14he distinguished himself with integrity, courage, and commitment; and 1.15WHEREAS, he married Ger Mary Thao in 1970 and together they had nine children. 1.16Throughout 55 years of marriage, he was a loving husband, devoted father, and proud grandfather 1.17of 23 grandchildren, all of whom he inspired to pursue education, character, and excellence; and 1.18WHEREAS, even in times of war, Za Xiong was unwavering in his pursuit of knowledge. 1.19He obtained his primary certificate, mastered English, trained in electronics with USAID, and later 1.20pursued higher education in America with unrelenting determination; and 1.21WHEREAS, he was featured in the Secret War documentary "The Child Soldiers of Vang 1.22Pao's Army" as the interpreter for a Hmong child soldier in Long Tien, Laos; and 1.23WHEREAS, following his resettlement to the United States in 1976, he achieved academic 1.24excellence, earning a GED and multiple other degrees, including a bachelor's of science, two 1.25associate's degrees, and a master's in administrative leadership, all while supporting his family and 1.26uplifting his community as a teacher, entrepreneur, and advocate; and 1.27WHEREAS, Za Xiong John Thao's life was a testament to sacrifice, resilience, and the power 1.28of education. He transformed hardship into hope and lived with humility, purpose, and enduring 1.29love for his family and people; and 1.30NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it 1.31honors the life and legacy of Za Xiong John Thao, eldest son, brave soldier, faithful scholar, and 1.32beloved father, in the hope that his story may continue to inspire generations to lead with courage, 1.33serve with heart, and rise with dignity. 1.34BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare an 1.35enrolled copy of this resolution, to be authenticated by the Secretary's signature and that of the 1.36Chair of the Senate Rules and Administration Committee, and transmit it to the family of Za Xiong 1.37John Thao.
