No. 78, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on April 21, 2026 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2expressing the sense of the Senate opposing the use of foreign steel in the construction 1.3of a ballroom at the White House. 1.4WHEREAS, recent reports indicate that construction activities at the White House include 1.5plans for a new ballroom; and 1.6WHEREAS, it has been reported that a portion of the steel designated for this project is being 1.7sourced from foreign producers; and 1.8WHEREAS, the use of foreign steel in federally significant projects raises concerns about 1.9consistency with long-standing national priorities to support domestic manufacturing and supply 1.10chains; and 1.11WHEREAS, Minnesota's Iron Range plays a critical role in supplying taconite for domestic 1.12steel production, supporting family-sustaining jobs and regional economic stability; and 1.13WHEREAS, demand for domestically produced steel is directly tied to the economic health 1.14of mining communities, where workers have experienced uncertainty and layoffs in recent years; 1.15and 1.16WHEREAS, the use of American-made steel in major construction projects reflects a 1.17commitment to supporting domestic industry, workforce development, and national economic 1.18security; and 1.19NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it 1.20opposes the use of foreign steel in the construction of a ballroom at the White House and urges 1.21federal decision-makers to prioritize the use of American-made steel in such projects. 1.22BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare a copy 1.23of this resolution, to be authenticated by his signature and that of the Chair of the Senate Rules and 1.24Administration Committee, and transmit it to the appropriate federal officials.
