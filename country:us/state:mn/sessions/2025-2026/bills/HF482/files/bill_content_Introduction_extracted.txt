BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin [168.1289] CHOOSE LIFE SPECIAL PLATES. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Issuance of plates. new text end new text begin The commissioner must issue Choose Life special license plates or a single motorcycle plate to an applicant who: new text end new text begin (1) is a registered owner of a passenger automobile, noncommercial one-ton pickup truck, motorcycle, or recreational vehicle; new text end new text begin (2) pays special plate fees under section 168.12, subdivision 5, for each set of plates, along with any other fees required by this chapter; new text end new text begin (3) pays the registration tax as required under section 168.013; new text end new text begin (4) pays the fees required under this chapter; new text end new text begin (5) contributes $25 upon initial application and a minimum of $25 annually to the Choose Life account; and new text end new text begin (6) complies with this chapter and rules governing registration of motor vehicles and licensing of drivers. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Design. new text end new text begin The commissioner must adopt a suitable plate design that includes an image of the Choose Life kids logo in the design area on the left side of the plate and the inscription "Choose Life" on the bottom of the plate. A second suitable logo that includes an image of a silhouette of one adult and one child must be adopted by the commissioner by April 1, 2026. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Plates transfer. new text end new text begin On application to the commissioner and payment of a transfer fee of $5, special plates may be transferred to another qualified motor vehicle that is registered to the same individual to whom the special plates were originally issued. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Exemption. new text end new text begin Special plates issued under this section are not subject to section 168.1293, subdivision 2. new text end new text begin Subd. 5. new text end new text begin Contributions; account; appropriation. new text end new text begin Contributions collected under subdivision 1, clause (5), must be deposited in the Choose Life account, which is established in the special revenue fund. Money in the account is appropriated to the commissioner of public safety. This appropriation is first for the annual cost of administering the account funds, and the remaining funds are for distribution to Choose Life Minnesota, Inc., to be distributed to agencies as required under subdivision 6. The commissioner must include with its distribution to Choose Life Minnesota, Inc., a report specifying the ratio of contributions collected under subdivision 1, clause (5), in each county to the total contributions collected in the state. new text end new text begin Subd. 6. new text end new text begin Contribution recipient requirements. new text end new text begin (a) Using the county contribution report from the commissioner in subdivision 5, Choose Life Minnesota, Inc., must distribute each county's share of collected contributions to nongovernmental, nonprofit agencies within each county that assist pregnant women who are making adoption plans for their children. Funds may not be distributed to any agency that is involved in or associated with abortion activities, including counseling for or referrals to abortion clinics, providing medical abortion-related procedures, or pro-abortion advertising. Funds may not be distributed to any agency that charges women for services received. new text end new text begin (b) Agencies that receive funds under this subdivision must use the funds to provide for the material needs of pregnant women who are making adoption plans for their children, including but not limited to clothing, housing, medical care, food, utilities, and transportation. Agencies that receive funds under this subdivision may not provide or assist a woman to obtain adoption services from a provider that is not licensed. Funds may be expended to support birth mothers for 60 days after delivery and infants awaiting placement with adoptive parents and any adoption-related counseling, training, or advertising. new text end new text begin (c) Funds distributed under this section must not be used for administrative expenses, legal expenses, or capital expenditures except that Choose Life Minnesota, Inc., may use up to 15 percent of funds for administration and promotion of the Choose Life special license plate program. new text end new text begin (d) If no qualified agency applies to receive funds in a county in any year, that county's share of contributions collected under subdivision 1, clause (5), must be distributed proportionally to any qualified agencies that apply to receive funds and that maintain a place of business within a 150-mile radius of the county seat of the county containing no qualified agency. If no qualified agencies apply, the funds must be held by Choose Life Minnesota, Inc., until a qualified agency under this subdivision applies for the funds. new text end new text begin (e) Each agency that receives funds under this subdivision must submit an annual attestation to Choose Life Minnesota, Inc. Any unused funds that exceed ten percent of the funds received by an agency each year must be returned to Choose Life Minnesota, Inc., which must distribute the funds to other qualified agencies within the state. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective January 1, 2026, for special plates issued on or after that date. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
