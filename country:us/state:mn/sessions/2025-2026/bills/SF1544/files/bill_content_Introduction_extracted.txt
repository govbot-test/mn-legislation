BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin MORATORIUM.new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Moratorium. new text end new text begin A moratorium is imposed on the final approval of applications for right-of-way and small wireless facility permits under Minnesota Statutes, section 237.163, and on the approval of utility construction permits and obstruction permits required to install a small wireless facility. The moratorium is for a period of one year beginning the day following final enactment of this section in order to conduct a study related to the health, economic, and environmental effects of 5th generation and next generation technology ("5G"). The moratorium does not affect previously completed small wireless facilities. An agency must continue to receive and review applications but must not approve or issue permits during the moratorium. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Report requirements. new text end new text begin (a) The commissioner of health must compile the report required under this subdivision. The commissioner of health must conduct a comprehensive study and evaluation regarding the health risks associated with 5G, including but not limited to the potential biological effects of electromagnetic radiation at higher frequencies. The commissioner must solicit input from affected or interested members of the public. The commissioner must view completed peer-reviewed research conducted in the United States and other countries. new text end new text begin (b) The commissioner of revenue, in collaboration with the commissioner of health, must evaluate the effects of 5G and tower placement on property values, specifically with respect to the potential devaluation of property values. new text end new text begin (c) The commissioner of natural resources, in collaboration with the commissioner of health, must study the impacts of 5G on the environment and wildlife. new text end new text begin (d) The commissioner of commerce must study the availability of adequate liability insurance for right-of-way users to protect against lawsuits that result from the adverse health or medical impacts of 5G technology. The commissioner of commerce must, in collaboration with the League of Minnesota Cities, develop a model ordinance after researching and evaluating best practices and ordinances in Minnesota and other states. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Report due date. new text end new text begin The report containing the findings and recommendations must be completed and submitted to the chairs and ranking minority members of the legislative committees with jurisdiction over health care policy, local government, and environmental issues by December 1, 2025. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Action based on report findings. new text end new text begin If the report under subdivision 3 finds that 5G technology results in adverse health effects and risks, the state may require that all 5G installations cease until the state develops a comprehensive policy to reduce the health effects and risks. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective the day following final enactment and applies to applications for right-of-way or small wireless facility permits filed or received on or after that date. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
