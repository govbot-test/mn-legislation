BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. Minnesota Statutes 2024, section 120A.41, is amended to read: 120A.41 LENGTH OF SCHOOL YEAR; HOURS OF INSTRUCTION. new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Calendar. new text end (a) A school board's annual school calendar must include at least 425 hours of instruction for a kindergarten student, 935 hours of instruction for a student in grades 1 through 6, and 1,020 hours of instruction for a student in grades 7 through 12, not including summer school. The school calendar for all-day kindergarten must include at least 850 hours of instruction for the school year. The school calendar for a prekindergarten student under section 142D.08, if offered by the district, must include at least 350 hours of instruction for the school year. A school board's annual calendar must include at least 165 days of instruction for a student in grades 1 through 11 unless a four-day week schedule has been approved by the commissioner under section 124D.126. (b) A school board's annual school calendar may include plans for up to five days of instruction provided through online instruction due to inclement weather. The inclement weather plans must be developed according to section 120A.414. new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Hours of instruction. new text end new text begin (a) Hours of instruction in a secondary school includes all educational experiences that: new text end new text begin (1) allow students to earn academic credit, as defined in section 120B.018; new text end new text begin (2) are available to all enrolled students; and new text end new text begin (3) are supervised, coordinated, and verified by a qualified teacher, as defined in section 122A.16. new text end new text begin (b) Educational experiences included in hours of instruction may: new text end new text begin (1) be included in any secondary school course of study prescribed by a school board under section 123B.09; and new text end new text begin (2) occur outside the regular school day and week, but not outside the school year. new text end new text begin (c) Nothing in this subdivision allows a district to deny a student access to any service or instruction required under state or federal law, including special education services. new text end new text begin (d) The Department of Education must regularly review and update its policies and structures, including district reporting requirements, to ensure that the department's policies and structures do not constrain districts from providing to students the range of educational opportunities available under this subdivision. new text end new text begin (e) Nothing in this subdivision modifies pupil units under chapter 126C, or provides additional pupil units for an educational experience included in hours of instruction. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective the day following final enactment. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
