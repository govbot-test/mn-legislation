BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin BUFFALO; PUBLIC UTILITIES COMMISSION.new text end new text begin (a) Notwithstanding Minnesota Statutes, sections 412.331 and 412.341, the city of Buffalo may establish a public utilities commission with five members by an ordinance that: new text end new text begin (1) expressly adopts the provisions of paragraph (b) in place of the provisions in Minnesota Statutes, section 412.341, subdivision 1; and new text end new text begin (2) otherwise complies with the requirements of Minnesota Statutes, section 412.331, including by expressly adopting all other provisions of Minnesota Statutes, sections 412.331 to 412.391. new text end new text begin (b) The commission shall consist of five members appointed by the council. No more than two members may be chosen from the council membership. Each member shall serve for a term of three years and until a successor is appointed and qualified except that of the members initially appointed in any city, two shall serve for a term of one year, two for a term of two years, and one for a term of three years. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective the day after the city of Buffalo and its chief clerical officer comply with the requirements of Minnesota Statutes, section 645.021, subdivisions 2 and 3. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
