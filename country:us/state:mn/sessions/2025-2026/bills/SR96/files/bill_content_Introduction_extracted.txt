No. 96, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on May 16, 2026 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2honoring the life and legacy of Lionel Rosenblatt. 1.3WHEREAS, Lionel Rosenblatt was a dedicated American diplomat and humanitarian who 1.4devoted his life to protecting individuals and families forced to flee war, violence, and persecution; 1.5and 1.6WHEREAS, during the era of the Vietnam War and the Secret War in Laos, the Hmong 1.7people of Laos stood as steadfast allies of the United States, with many Hmong men serving 1.8courageously alongside American-backed forces; and 1.9WHEREAS, following the fall of Laos to the Pathet Lao in 1975, Hmong families faced 1.10persecution, retaliation, and displacement, forcing thousands to flee their homeland and seek refuge 1.11in camps across Thailand; and 1.12WHEREAS, Lionel Rosenblatt, while serving as refugee coordinator at the United States 1.13Embassy in Bangkok, recognized the moral obligation of the United States to its Hmong allies and 1.14worked tirelessly to ensure their protection, safety, and resettlement; and 1.15WHEREAS, in the face of doubt and discrimination, he demonstrated courage and conviction 1.16by advocating for Hmong refugees, challenging unjust barriers, and creating pathways for thousands 1.17of families to build new lives in the United States; and 1.18WHEREAS, Lionel Rosenblatt's unwavering belief that those who stood with America in 1.19times of conflict should not be abandoned reflects the highest ideals of justice, loyalty, and 1.20humanitarian service; and 1.21WHEREAS, thanks in part to his leadership and advocacy, Hmong Americans have flourished 1.22and continue to make lasting contributions to the social, cultural, economic, and civic life of 1.23communities across the nation; and 1.24WHEREAS, Lionel Rosenblatt's legacy serves as a lasting reminder that true leadership 1.25requires compassion, integrity, and the courage to act when it matters most; and 1.26WHEREAS, Lionel Rosenblatt passed away on April 11, 2026, in Washington, D.C., at the 1.27age of 82, leaving behind a profound legacy of service and humanity; and 1.28NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it 1.29honors the life and legacy of Lionel Rosenblatt, recognizes his extraordinary contributions to the 1.30Hmong community and all refugees, and reaffirms the commitment of the Senate to the values he 1.31championed: standing with allies, welcoming those in need, and upholding dignity and justice for 1.32all. 1.33BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare an 1.34enrolled copy of this resolution, to be authenticated by the Secretary's signature and that of the 1.35Chair of the Senate Rules and Administration Committee, and transmit it to the family of Lionel 1.36Rosenblatt.
