BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin PERMANENT SCHOOL TRUST LANDS TASK FORCE.new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Task force established. new text end new text begin A task force is established to examine the permanent school trust lands located within or near Tribal lands. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Membership qualifications and appointments. new text end new text begin (a) Appointed members of the task force must have familiarity in at least one of the following areas: new text end new text begin (1) Tribal lands; new text end new text begin (2) American Indian education; new text end new text begin (3) state and Tribal relations; new text end new text begin (4) Tribal treaties; new text end new text begin (5) state land management; new text end new text begin (6) forest resources management; or new text end new text begin (7) permanent school trust land management policies. new text end new text begin (b) The task force consists of the following members, each of whom must be appointed by August 1, 2026: new text end new text begin (1) one member appointed by each of the six Tribes with permanent school trust lands located within that Tribe's Tribal lands boundaries; new text end new text begin (2) at the discretion of each Tribe, one member appointed by each Tribe without permanent school trust lands located within that Tribe's Tribal lands boundaries; new text end new text begin (3) one member appointed by the Minnesota Indian Affairs Council; new text end new text begin (4) one member appointed by the Tribal Nations Education Committee; new text end new text begin (5) the commissioner of education or the commissioner's designee; new text end new text begin (6) one member appointed by the commissioner of natural resources; new text end new text begin (7) the director of the Office of School Trust Lands; new text end new text begin (8) one member appointed by the Minnesota Forest Resources Council; new text end new text begin (9) one member appointed by vote of the Legislative Permanent School Fund Commission; and new text end new text begin (10) four members appointed by the legislature, one each by the majority leader of the senate, the minority leader of the senate, the speaker of the house, and the Democratic-Farmer-Labor caucus leader of the house of representatives. new text end new text begin (c) The first meeting of the task force must be called by the Legislative Coordinating Commission no later than September 1, 2026. The task force must select its chair at the first meeting. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Consultation and advice. new text end new text begin The Departments of Natural Resources and Education must provide advice and technical assistance as necessary to the task force. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Duties. new text end new text begin The task force must examine policies for land exchanges of permanent school trust lands located within the boundaries of Tribal lands, identify obstacles to land transfers, and propose effective land transfer mechanisms. The task force must also examine land use and land management policies for permanent school trust lands located near the boundaries of Tribal lands. The task force must make recommendations for funding sources to accomplish any land transfers recommended by the task force. new text end new text begin Subd. 5. new text end new text begin Compensation. new text end new text begin Minnesota Statutes, section 15.059, subdivision 3, governs compensation of the members of the task force. new text end new text begin Subd. 6. new text end new text begin Meetings and administrative support. new text end new text begin The Legislative Coordinating Commission must provide meeting space and technical, organizational, administrative, and staff support for the task force. Meetings of the task force are subject to Minnesota Statutes, section 3.055. The meetings may be conducted by interactive television. new text end new text begin Subd. 7. new text end new text begin Task force report. new text end new text begin The task force must report its recommendations to the chairs and ranking minority members of the legislative committees with jurisdiction over the permanent school fund by February 15, 2027. The task force report must be submitted consistent with Minnesota Statutes, section 3.195. The task force expires on February 15, 2027, or upon submission of the report required under this subdivision, whichever occurs earlier. new text end new text begin Subd. 8. new text end new text begin Appropriations. new text end new text begin (a) $....... in fiscal year 2027 is appropriated from the general fund to the Legislative Coordinating Commission to administer the permanent school trust lands task force. new text end new text begin (b) $....... in fiscal year 2027 is appropriated from the general fund to the Department of Natural Resources for technical agency support for the task force. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
