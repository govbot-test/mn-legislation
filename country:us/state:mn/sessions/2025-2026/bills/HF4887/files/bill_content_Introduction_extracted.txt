BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin [504B.213] RIGHT TO TERMINATE LEASE UPON LOSS OF INCOME. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Definitions. new text end new text begin (a) For purposes of this section, the following terms have the meanings given. new text end new text begin (b) "Income" means salary, wages, tips, commissions, and professional fees. new text end new text begin (c) "Qualifying document" means: new text end new text begin (1) documentation by the tenant's employer of termination of the tenant's employment or reduction in income; new text end new text begin (2) documentation of the tenant's loss or reduction of income from a source of the income, other than an employer; or new text end new text begin (3) if documentation under clause (1) or (2) is unavailable, a statement by the tenant or the authorized representative of the tenant that includes the following: new text end new text begin (i) a description of the tenant's loss or reduction of income and inability to pay the rent based on the loss or reduction of income; new text end new text begin (ii) the reason the documentation under clause (1) or (2) is unavailable; and new text end new text begin (iii) an attestation that the statement is true and correct. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Termination of lease upon loss of income; notice. new text end new text begin (a) A residential tenant or the authorized representative of the tenant may terminate a lease prior to the expiration of the lease if: new text end new text begin (1) the tenant lost income or their income was reduced and the tenant is unable to pay rent because of the loss or reduction of income; new text end new text begin (2) the tenant or the tenant's authorized representative provides written notice to the landlord at least 14 days prior to the proposed date of termination of the lease; and new text end new text begin (3) a qualifying document is delivered with the written notice. new text end new text begin (b) The tenant or the tenant's authorized representative must deliver the written notice and qualifying document to the landlord by mail, in person, or by a form of written communication the tenant regularly uses to communicate with the landlord. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Date of termination; liability for rent. new text end new text begin If the tenant complies with the requirements under subdivision 2, the lease is terminated on the proposed date of termination provided in the written notice. The termination of a lease under this section shall not relieve the tenant from liability for the payment of rent or other sums owed prior to the date of termination of the lease, including during the notice period. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Waiver prohibited. new text end new text begin A waiver of the rights provided under this section, including a provision that requires a longer notice period than that provided in this section, is contrary to public policy and is void and unenforceable. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective the day following final enactment and applies to leases entered into, renewed, or extended on or after that date. For the purposes of this section, estates at will shall be deemed to be renewed or extended at the commencement of each rental period. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
