BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin [125A.071] MONITORING AND ENFORCEMENT OF SPECIAL EDUCATION CASE LOADS, CLASS SIZE, AND WORKLOAD. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Case loads, class size, and workload requirements for school-age students receiving special education. new text end new text begin (a) A district must limit the number of school-age students that are assigned to a classroom as follows: new text end new text begin (1) for students who receive direct special instruction from a teacher more than 60 percent of the instructional day, but less than a full school day: new text end new text begin (i) for students identified as deafblind, or as having autism spectrum disorders or developmental cognitive disability, with a severe-profound range or severe multiple impairments, no more than three students per adult; new text end new text begin (ii) for students identified as having autism spectrum disorders or developmental cognitive disability, with a mild-moderate range or a specific learning disability, no more than 12 students per teacher or 15 students with one or more paraprofessionals; new text end new text begin (iii) for students identified as having any other disabilities, no more than ten students with one paraprofessional or 12 students with two paraprofessionals; and new text end new text begin (iv) for students who receive special education services for 60 percent or more of the instructional day, and who are highly disruptive or create an unsafe environment due to the high behavioral or mental health needs of the students, districts must reduce the number of such students in the classroom so that all students and staff are safe; and new text end new text begin (2) for students who receive direct special education for a full school day: new text end new text begin (i) for students identified as deafblind, or as having autism spectrum disorders or developmental cognitive disability, with a severe-profound range or severe multiple impairments, no more than four students with one paraprofessional, except that for each additional paraprofessional, two more students may be added in the classroom; and new text end new text begin (ii) for students with all other disabilities, no more than eight students with one paraprofessional, except that for each additional paraprofessional in the classroom, two more students may be added. new text end new text begin (b) For students who receive direct special education 60 percent or less of the instructional day, and for students who receive related services as part of an individualized education program (IEP), the school district must establish a board-approved policy for determining workload limits for special education staff, including speech-language pathologists, physical therapists, occupational therapists, art therapists, music therapists, audiologists, school psychologists, school counselors, developmental adapted physical education teachers, and school nurses who provide IEP services. The policy must be based on student contact minutes, evaluation and reevaluation time, indirect services, IEPs managed, travel time, and other services required in the IEPs of eligible students. If the special education staff are represented by an exclusive representative, the policy must be negotiated as part of the collective bargaining agreement between the exclusive representative and the school board. new text end new text begin (c) Notwithstanding any law to the contrary, a collective bargaining agreement may not provide for adult-to-student ratios in classrooms or student-to-personnel ratios that allow more students in a classroom than permitted under this section. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Case loads for early childhood program alternatives. new text end new text begin (a) A district must adjust downward a teacher's case load based on the children's severity of disability or delay, travel time necessary to serve children in more than one program alternative, and if the children in the teacher's case loads are receiving services in more than one program alternative or the children are involved with other agencies. A teacher may be assigned to manage a maximum of: new text end new text begin (1) 12 children ages birth through two years per one teacher; new text end new text begin (2) 16 children ages three through six years per one teacher; and new text end new text begin (3) 14 children ages birth through six years per one teacher. new text end new text begin (b) A district must employ at least one paraprofessional per early childhood special education class while children are in attendance. The maximum number of children in an early childhood special education classroom at any one time with a teacher and a paraprofessional is eight. The maximum number of children in an early childhood special education classroom at any one time with an early childhood team is 16. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Complaint form. new text end new text begin The Department of Education must post on its website a form that a parent, teacher, or member of the public may file with the department alleging that case loads, class size, or workloads in a school district exceed the limits established under this section. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Investigation. new text end new text begin (a) Within 60 days of receiving a complaint, the Division of Assistance and Compliance at the Department of Education must complete an investigation into the complaint that case loads, class size, or workloads exceed the limits established under this section. new text end new text begin (b) Upon completing the investigation, the division must provide its written findings to the complainant and to the school district that is the subject of the complaint. new text end new text begin (c) If the division determines that case loads, class size, or workloads within a school district exceed the limits in this section, the division must provide the school district with reasonable time to comply with the case load limits. new text end new text begin (d) The division must follow up with the school district after the reasonable time provided to the district under paragraph (c) has passed. If the school district remains out of compliance, the division must notify the parent or guardian of all affected students. new text end new text begin Subd. 5. new text end new text begin Summary of findings. new text end new text begin The department must post on its website a summary of its findings in any investigation finding that case loads, class size, or workloads in a school district or charter school exceed the limits established under this section. The findings must redact or omit any information not otherwise public under chapter 13. new text end new text begin Subd. 6. new text end new text begin Effect of noncompliance. new text end new text begin (a) A collective bargaining agreement between a school board and the exclusive representative of teachers or paraprofessionals in the district must include remedies for noncompliance with this section. new text end new text begin (b) Upon issuance of a determination by the Division of Assistance and Compliance that a district has exceeded the limits under this section, the district must comply with the remedies established in the applicable collective bargaining agreement. new text end new text begin (c) If the teachers or special education staff in the district are not represented by an exclusive representative, the district must consult with the teachers and special education staff regarding an appropriate remedy to address the determination that the district has exceeded the limits under this section. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective July 1, 2026. new text end Sec. 2. new text begin REPEALER.new text end new text begin Minnesota Rules, part 3525.2340, subparts 4 and 5, new text end new text begin are repealed. new text end APPENDIX Repealed Minnesota Rule: 26-08091 3525.2340 CASE LOADS. § Subp. 4. Case loads for school-age educational service alternatives. § A. The maximum number of school-age pupils that may be assigned to a teacher: § (1) for pupils who receive direct special instruction from a teacher more than 60 percent of the instructional day, but less than a full school day: § (a) deafblind, autism spectrum disorders, developmental cognitive disability: severe-profound range, or severely multiply impaired, three pupils; § (b) deafblind, autism spectrum disorders, developmental cognitive disability: severe-profound range, or severely multiply impaired with one paraprofessional, six pupils; § (c) developmental cognitive disability: mild-moderate range or specific learning disabled, 12 pupils; § (d) developmental cognitive disability: mild-moderate range or specific learning disabled with one paraprofessional, 15 pupils; § (e) all other disabilities with one paraprofessional, ten pupils; § (f) all other disabilities with two paraprofessionals, 12 pupils; and § (g) under special circumstances, for children who receive special education services for 60 percent or more of the instructional day, that are highly disruptive or create an unsafe environment due to the high behavioral or mental health needs of the students, districts have the option of lowering the number of such students in the classroom, so that both students and staff are safe; and § (2) for pupils who receive direct special education for a full day: § (a) deafblind, autism spectrum disorders, developmental cognitive disability: severe-profound range, or severely multiply impaired with one paraprofessional, four pupils; § (b) deafblind, autism spectrum disorders, developmental cognitive disability: severe-profound range, or severely multiply impaired with two paraprofessionals, six pupils; and § (c) all other disabilities with one paraprofessional, eight pupils. § B. For pupils who receive direct special education 60 percent or less of the instructional day, the school district must establish a board-approved policy for determining workload limits for special education staff based on student contact minutes, evaluation and reevaluation time, indirect services, IEPs managed, travel time, and other services required in the IEPs of eligible students. § Subp. 5. Case loads for early childhood program alternatives. A teacher's case load must be adjusted downward based on pupils' severity of disability or delay, travel time necessary to serve pupils in more than one program alternative, and if the pupils on the teacher's case loads are receiving services in more than one program alternative or the pupils are involved with other agencies. The maximum number of pupils that can be assigned to a teacher in any early childhood program alternative is: § A. birth through two years: 12 pupils per teacher; § B. three through six years: 16 pupils per teacher; and § C. birth through six years: 14 pupils per teacher. District early childhood special education (ECSE) classes must have at least one paraprofessional employed while pupils are in attendance. The maximum number of pupils in an ECSE classroom at any one time with a teacher and a paraprofessional is eight. The maximum number of pupils in an ECSE classroom at any one time with an early childhood team is 16.
