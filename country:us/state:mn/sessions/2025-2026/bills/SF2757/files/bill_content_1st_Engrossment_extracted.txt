BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin LOCAL GOVERNMENT GRANTS; LEGAL EXPENSES.new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Definition. new text end new text begin For purposes of this section, "local government" means a county, home rule charter or statutory city, township, or school district. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Appropriation. new text end new text begin $100,000,000 in fiscal year 2026 is appropriated from the general fund to the commissioner of revenue to make grants to local governments for eligible costs in subdivision 4. The commissioner of revenue may transfer money in the local government grant account to the commissioner of education to distribute grants to school districts that have been awarded a grant under subdivision 3. This is a onetime appropriation and is available until June 30, 2027. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Application; award. new text end new text begin A local government may apply to the commissioner of revenue for a grant to pay for eligible costs under subdivision 4. A local government may apply to the commissioner for a grant in a form and manner prescribed by the commissioner. The commissioner must allocate grants on a first-come, first-served basis. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Eligible costs. new text end new text begin Grants under this section must be used to reimburse a local government for eligible costs incurred by a local government. Eligible costs include: new text end new text begin (1) increased administrative costs for programs based on federal changes; new text end new text begin (2) amounts for infrastructure projects that have been canceled or withheld by the federal government; new text end new text begin (3) amounts of expected federal funds that have been canceled or withheld by federal executive order; new text end new text begin (4) costs associated with recovery from Operation Metro Surge; and new text end new text begin (5) legal costs associated with upholding state or local laws that conflict with federal executive orders made effective after January 19, 2025. new text end new text begin No individual grant to a local government may exceed $2,500,000. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective the day following final enactment. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
