No. 92, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on May 16, 2026 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2recognizing September 1, 2025, as Hmong Minnesota Day at the Minnesota 1.3State Fair. 1.4WHEREAS, the Minnesota State Fair first opened in 1859 and, with few exceptions, has 1.5been held annually as a festive gathering where Minnesotans showcase agriculture, food, competition, 1.6rides, merchandise, and industry; and 1.7WHEREAS, Hmong, the original highland people of Laos, were recruited to fight for the 1.8United States during the Secret War in Laos from 1961 to 1975; and 1.9WHEREAS, after America withdrew from the Vietnam War, Hmong people became the 1.10target of government persecution and thousands escaped to the United States and other countries; 1.11and 1.12WHEREAS, following the 1975 Vietnam conflict, thousands of Laotian-Hmong allied with 1.13the United States were resettled as refugees in western countries, with Minnesota now home to at 1.14least 94,000 Hmong-Americans who continue to honor their heritage; and 1.15WHEREAS, 2025 marks the 50th anniversary of the resettlement of Southeast Asian refugees, 1.16including Hmong, Lao, Vietnamese, Cambodian, and other communities, whose arrival in Minnesota 1.17beginning in 1975 has profoundly enriched the cultural, civic, and economic life of our state; and 1.18WHEREAS, the Hmong community in Minnesota continues to show resilience and pride 1.19in their cultural heritage, celebrating "50 Years Strong" at the tenth annual Hmong Minnesota State 1.20Fair Day, a milestone that honors their journey from resettlement to becoming an integral part of 1.21the state's cultural and civic life; and 1.22NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it 1.23commemorates Hmong Minnesota Day at the Minnesota State Fair on September 1, 2025.
