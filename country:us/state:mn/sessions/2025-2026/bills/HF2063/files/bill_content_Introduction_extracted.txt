BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin [10.501] 1ST MINNESOTA DAY. new text end new text begin (a) July 2 of each year is designated 1st Minnesota Day for the purpose of honoring the 262 men of the 1st Minnesota Regiment who fought for the Union on July 2, 1863, responding to General Winfield S. Hancock's order to plug a gap in the Union line by charging at the double-quick with fixed bayonets. In the few minutes of the charge, 215 men were killed. The charge allowed other units to reach and close the gap. The battle and the sacrifice of these men was an important contribution in saving the Union. new text end new text begin (b) Each year the governor shall issue a proclamation honoring this observance. new text end new text begin (c) Schools are encouraged to offer instruction on the role of the 1st Minnesota Regiment during the Civil War, including but not limited to sharing the stories of those who fought to save the Union for freedom and democracy against secession. new text end new text begin (d) On July 2 of each year, the commissioner of administration, in cooperation with the Minnesota Historical Society, must fly the Minnesota Historical Society's replica of the 1st Minnesota Regimental flag on the North flag pole of the Minnesota State Capitol in place of the POW/MIA flag. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective the day following final enactment. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
