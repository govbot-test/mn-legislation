No. 66, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on February 26, 2026 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2expressing the sense of the Senate condemning Hinduphobia and anti-Hindu bigotry, 1.3and reaffirming Minnesota's commitment to religious freedom, inclusion, and mutual 1.4respect. 1.5WHEREAS, the State of Minnesota, guided by its state constitution and the Minnesota Human 1.6Rights Act, has a long, proud history of defending religious freedom without fear of intimidation 1.7or persecution. The people of Minnesota, through the United States Constitution and its traditions, 1.8are committed to the principles of religious liberty, freedom of expression, cultural pluralism, and 1.9the inherent dignity of every individual; and 1.10WHEREAS, Hinduism, also known as "Sanatana Dharma," is one of the world's oldest and 1.11largest religions, with over 1.2 billion adherents globally and a diverse and growing community of 1.12over four million in the United States, who have contributed immeasurably to our society. The 1.13Hindu community faces challenges, including discrimination, human rights violations, and 1.14marginalization, which underscores the importance of affirming their dignity and rights everywhere; 1.15and 1.16WHEREAS, the Hindu-American community has been a major contributor to diverse sectors, 1.17such as yoga, ayurveda, meditation, medicine, science and engineering, information technology, 1.18hospitality, finance, academia, manufacturing, energy, retail trade, and much more, enriching the 1.19cultural, economic, and social fabric of our state and billions globally; and 1.20WHEREAS, Hinduphobia, as described in the Understanding Hinduphobia initiative, is "a 1.21set of antagonistic, destructive, and derogatory attitudes and behaviors toward Hinduism (Sanatana 1.22Dharma) and Hindus that may manifest as prejudice, fear, or hatred" leading to discrimination, 1.23harassment, and violence; and 2.1WHEREAS, in 2022, a study released by Rutgers University titled "Anti-Hindu 2.2Disinformation: A Case Study of Hinduphobia on Social Media" detailed how hate messages in 2.3social media translate into real-world threats against the Hindu community; and 2.4WHEREAS, hate-based vandalism against Hindu places of worship (mandirs) is rising 2.5nationally, with recent incidents in New Jersey, Indiana, California, New York, and North Carolina. 2.6Bullets tore through an ISKCON mandir in Utah. In Minnesota, the Maple Grove mandir, one of 2.7the largest in the country, has experienced acts of vandalism, and the mandir in Edina has been the 2.8target of hate speech. These incidents reflect a spreading wave of Hinduphobia that calls for 2.9recognition and protection; and 2.10WHEREAS, bipartisan cooperation and leadership are essential in upholding these shared 2.11values and in combating any form of religious discrimination or bigotry that undermines social 2.12cohesion and democratic ideals; NOW, THEREFORE, 2.13BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it unequivocally condemns 2.14Hinduphobia, anti-Hindu bigotry, and all forms of religious intolerance. 2.15BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that Minnesota welcomes the diversity and contributions of 2.16its Hindu-American residents and is committed to protecting their right to worship in safety and 2.17peace. 2.18BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that Minnesota encourages state and local agencies, including 2.19educational institutions and law enforcement, to enhance their understanding of Hinduphobia and 2.20to include the Hindu community in their diversity initiatives, anti-bias training programs, and 2.21interfaith dialogue. 2.22BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare copies 2.23of this resolution, to be authenticated by his signature and that of the Chair of the Senate Rules and 2.24Administration Committee.
