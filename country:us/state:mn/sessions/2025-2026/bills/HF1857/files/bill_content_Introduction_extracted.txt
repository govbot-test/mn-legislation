BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin [343.256] ELECTIVE DECLAWING PROHIBITED. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Definitions. new text end new text begin (a) For purposes of this section, the terms in this subdivision have the meanings given. new text end new text begin (b) "Cat" means an animal of the taxonomic family Felidae. new text end new text begin (c) "Declawing" means an onychectomy or any other surgical procedure to amputate or modify a portion of a cat's paw in order to remove the cat's claws. Declawing does not include the trimming of nonviable claw husk or placing nonpermanent nail caps. new text end new text begin (d) "Procedure performed solely for a therapeutic purpose" means a medically necessary procedure to address an existing or recurring infection, disease, injury, or abnormal condition in the claws, nail bed, or toe bone, that jeopardizes the cat's health. Procedure performed solely for a therapeutic purpose does not include a procedure performed for a cosmetic or aesthetic purpose or to make a cat more convenient to keep or handle. new text end new text begin (e) "Tendonectomy" means a procedure in which the tendons to a cat's limbs, paws, or toes are cut or modified so that the normal functioning of the claws is impaired. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Prohibited acts. new text end new text begin Except as provided in subdivision 3, a person must not perform surgical claw removal, declawing, or a tendonectomy on any cat or otherwise alter a cat's toes, claws, or paws in a manner that prevents or impairs the normal function of the cat's toes, claws, or paws. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Therapeutic purpose. new text end new text begin The prohibition in subdivision 2 does not apply to a procedure performed solely for a therapeutic purpose. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Civil penalties. new text end new text begin A person who violates this section is subject to a civil penalty of $500 for the first violation, $1,000 for the second violation, and $2,500 for the third and any subsequent violation. Civil penalties may be recovered by a civil action brought by the county attorney or the attorney general in the name of the state. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
