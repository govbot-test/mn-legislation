No. 82, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on May 4, 2026 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2commending Jeff Brown for his service as a police officer. 1.3WHEREAS, those civic-minded individuals who work tirelessly in their communities perform 1.4an important service deserving of special legislative recognition; and 1.5WHEREAS, after working 30 years in law enforcement, Officer Jeff Brown is retiring; and 1.6WHEREAS, Jeff began his law enforcement career in 1995 as a police reserve officer with 1.7the Champlin Police Department. In 1997, he was hired by the Minnetrista Police Department as 1.8a police officer. In 2003, Jeff left Minnetrista and was hired by the Champlin Police Department; 1.9and 1.10WHEREAS, during his career, Jeff was a D.A.R.E officer for five years and a school resource 1.11officer for over three years He worked hand-in-hand with S.A.D.D. and M.A.D.D to promote 1.12awareness of drunk driving. He worked tirelessly with high students to promote seat belt use and 1.13his groups of students were two-time winners of the Hennepin County Golden Seat Belt Challenge. 1.14As a field training officer, Jeff worked with new recruits and showed them the ropes in an 1.15ever-changing line of work; and 1.16WHEREAS, Jeff was a member of the bike patrol unit, an investigator, a union steward, and 1.17an organizer of numerous law enforcement-related public events. In 1998, Jeff organized his first 1.18Police Citizens Academy. Since that time, he has continued to host the class every spring. He 1.19enjoyed working with fellow officers to educate the public about what it is like to be a police officer. 1.20Jeff always valued forming strong relationships with citizens in the community; and 1.21WHEREAS, in 2004, Jeff took over the Champlin Police Chaplain Corps. Today, the corps 1.22has grown to include the cities of Brooklyn Park and Osseo. Jeff was the Champlin Police Traffic 1.23Car for several years and was a vigilant enforcement presence throughout the city of Champlin; 1.24and 2.1WHEREAS, the next chapter of Jeff's life will include plenty of bow hunting in Wisconsin, 2.2Kansas, and Missouri; working on old muscle cars; and spending more time with his children, 2.3grandchildren, and Janice, his wife of 39 years; and 2.4WHEREAS, a retirement ceremony for Jeff will be held Friday, July 11, at the Champlin 2.5Police Department; and 2.6NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it 2.7congratulates Jeff Brown on his police career and wishes him well in retirement. 2.8BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare an 2.9enrolled copy of this resolution, to be authenticated by the Secretary's signature and that of the 2.10Chair of the Senate Rules and Administration Committee, and transmit it to Officer Jeff Brown.
