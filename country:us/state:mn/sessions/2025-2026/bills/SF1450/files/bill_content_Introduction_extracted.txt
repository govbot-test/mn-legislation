BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin [327C.098] ACQUISITION OF CERTAIN MANUFACTURED HOME PARKS. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Definitions. new text end new text begin (a) For the purposes of this section, the following terms have the meanings given. new text end new text begin (b) "Private equity company" means a publicly traded or nonpublicly traded entity that collects capital investments from individuals or entities. Private equity company includes a hedge fund; any entity that would be considered an investment company as defined in United States Code, title 15, section 80a-3, but for the application of paragraph (1) or (7) of subsection (c) of that section; a venture capital fund, as defined in Code of Federal Regulations, title 17, section 275.203(l)-1; a sovereign wealth fund; and any affiliated company or person that directly, or through an affiliate, acts as a control person. new text end new text begin (c) "Ownership or control" means the assumption of governance or the acquisition of an ownership interest, or direct or indirect control by a private equity company over the operations of a manufactured home park through any means, including but not limited to a purchase, lease, transfer, exchange, option, conveyance, creation of a joint venture, or any other manner of acquisition of assets, governance, an ownership interest, or direct or indirect control of a manufactured home park. new text end new text begin (d) "Real estate investment trust" has the meaning given in United States Code, title 26, section 856. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Notice and approval. new text end new text begin (a) A park owner must provide written notice to the attorney general of the intent to transfer ownership or control of the manufactured home park to a private equity company 120 days prior to the transfer of ownership or control. new text end new text begin (b) Together with the notice, the private equity company seeking to acquire ownership or control of the manufactured home park shall provide the attorney general with the following information: new text end new text begin (1) the names of each individual with an interest in the company and the percentage of interest each individual holds in the company; new text end new text begin (2) the complete corporate structure of the company; new text end new text begin (3) the names of each individual holding, and the percent of interest held, in any affiliate, subsidiary, or otherwise related entity with which the company has a contract to provide goods or services that will be used in the operation or maintenance of the manufactured home park or that will be provided to residents, including any real estate investment trusts if permitted under subdivision 6; new text end new text begin (4) for the previous five years, any filings that were required to be made to any agency of the federal government or a state government; new text end new text begin (5) evidence as to the condition of the water infrastructure of the park that the private equity company seeks to acquire or control, including but not limited to the park's distribution, sewer, and septic systems and the park's water quality; and new text end new text begin (6) any other information the attorney general may require. new text end new text begin (c) Together with the notice, the private equity company seeking to acquire ownership or control of the manufactured home park shall submit an affidavit to the attorney general, accompanied by evidence, sufficient to demonstrate that: new text end new text begin (1) the private equity company has the financial, managerial, and operational ability to operate or manage the manufactured home park consistent with the other requirements of this chapter, chapters 327 and 327B, and any other applicable Minnesota law and rule; new text end new text begin (2) neither the company nor any of the owners, officers, or any employees of the company have ever been convicted of a crime or found civilly liable for an offense involving moral turpitude, including forgery, embezzlement, obtaining money under false pretenses, larceny, extortion, conspiracy to defraud, or any other similar offense or violation, or any violation of a federal or state law or regulation relating to any consumer fraud, false advertising, deceptive trade practices, or similar consumer protection law; new text end new text begin (3) in the preceding ten years, there have been no judgments or completed or pending private or public litigation, tax liens, written complaints, administrative actions, or investigations by any government agency against the private equity company or any of its owners, officials, or employees, unresolved or otherwise, filed or otherwise commenced; new text end new text begin (4) in the preceding ten years, the company has not defaulted in the payment of money collected for others, including the discharge of debts through bankruptcy proceedings; new text end new text begin (5) the company will invest sufficient capital in the manufactured home park to maintain the park's infrastructure, if it is adequate, or to improve the park's infrastructure if it is deteriorating, in disrepair, or needs upgrading; new text end new text begin (6) after acquiring ownership or control of a manufactured home park anywhere in the United States, rents have not increased in any of the manufactured home parks in any one year by more than the Consumer Price Index published by the United States Department of Labor for the 12 months preceding the month in which the increase became effective or, if the private equity company has increased rents or prices by more than the Consumer Price Index, there has been a reasonable justification for the increase; and new text end new text begin (7) within five years after acquiring ownership or control of any other manufactured home park anywhere in the United States, the private equity company has not sold or otherwise transferred ownership or control to another person or entity. new text end new text begin (d) Ownership or control of a manufactured home park may not be transferred to a private equity company unless approved by the attorney general. Approval may be granted only if the attorney general finds that the transfer will not: new text end new text begin (1) result in an adverse impact on the health, safety, and well-being of the residents of the manufactured home park; new text end new text begin (2) lead to unaffordable rent increases for the residents; new text end new text begin (3) lead to a reduction in the quality of services provided to the residents; and new text end new text begin (4) lead to a reduction in maintenance or a deterioration in the operations and of the infrastructure of the park, if in adequate condition. new text end new text begin (e) If the attorney general does not issue an approval or denial within 120 days of receipt of the notice of intent to acquire ownership or control of a manufactured home park, approval is deemed to have been given. If the notice is incomplete or additional information is requested by the attorney general, the 120-day period may be extended by an additional 60 days. new text end new text begin (f) In addition to the requirements under this section for approval of the acquisition, the attorney general may impose any other requirements deemed necessary to protect the residents of the manufactured home park or protect the public interest. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Conditional approval. new text end new text begin (a) The attorney general may waive the notice period and issue immediate, conditional approval if: new text end new text begin (1) the health, safety, and well-being of the residents are in immediate jeopardy if the transfer of ownership or control is not immediately effectuated; or new text end new text begin (2) the owner of the manufactured home park can demonstrate a substantial likelihood that, absent the waiver, it will have to file for bankruptcy under chapter 11 of the Bankruptcy Act, United States Code, title 11, section 1101, et seq., or that it is at imminent risk of liquidation under chapter 7 of the Bankruptcy Act, United States Code, title 11, section 701, et seq. new text end new text begin (b) Within 90 days of issuing conditional approval, the attorney general must determine whether approval shall be permanent or if approval shall be withdrawn. If permanent approval is withdrawn for acquisition of ownership or control of a manufactured home park, the attorney general shall appoint a receiver and apply to the district court in the county in which the manufactured home park is located for confirmation of the appointment. The district court shall have exclusive jurisdiction to determine all matters related to the receivership. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Appeal of denial. new text end new text begin A private equity company that has been denied approval to acquire ownership or control of a manufactured home park may, within 30 days of receipt of the notice of denial, file an appeal with the Office of Administrative Hearings. Upon receipt of an appeal, the Office of Administrative Hearings must, at the discretion of the chief administrative judge, conduct a hearing or refer the matter for a contested case proceeding under chapter 14. new text end new text begin Subd. 5. new text end new text begin Prohibited practice. new text end new text begin A private equity company acquiring ownership or control of a manufactured home park is prohibited from engaging in any act, practice, or course of business that would strip an asset from the manufactured home park or otherwise undermine the quality or safety of, or access to, care and services. new text end new text begin Subd. 6. new text end new text begin Limitation on the use of real estate investment trusts. new text end new text begin A private equity company may enter into an arrangement with a real estate investment trust in connection with the manufactured home park only if it can demonstrate to the attorney general that: new text end new text begin (1) any rent increases to residents would not exceed the average of increases in market rent for residential multifamily properties in the market area in which the manufactured home park is situated; and new text end new text begin (2) the private equity company's acquisition of the manufactured home park would not cause hardship or have an unduly adverse impact on residents and their ability to afford housing. new text end new text begin Subd. 7. new text end new text begin Reports to the attorney general. new text end new text begin Thirteen months after the acquisition of ownership or control of a manufactured home park by a private equity company and every 12 months thereafter, the company must submit a report to the attorney general on: new text end new text begin (1) the financial status of the company and any other covered entities; new text end new text begin (2) impacts and outcomes on the safety of residents and the affordability of rent for residents; new text end new text begin (3) any filings required to be made to any agency of the federal government; new text end new text begin (4) the annual balance sheet of the company; new text end new text begin (5) the total dollar amount of aggregate fees and expenses collected by the private equity company, the manager of the company, and related parties, categorized by the type of fee and purpose of the fee; new text end new text begin (6) any management services agreements or sales and leaseback arrangements between the company and any affiliated or nonaffiliated companies and an itemization by category and amount of the fees paid; new text end new text begin (7) any services procured by the company from any other company owned by or affiliated with the company; new text end new text begin (8) all political spending by the company, including contributions and lobbying spending to members of or candidates for the Minnesota legislature, and with respect to issues affecting manufactured home parks; and new text end new text begin (9) the total number of manufactured home parks operating in Minnesota owned or controlled by the company and the names and locations of each manufactured home park. new text end Sec. 2. new text begin ATTORNEY GENERAL INVESTIGATION.new text end new text begin (a) The attorney general shall investigate the impact on residents and the state of the acquisition of ownership and control of manufactured home parks by private equity companies, as that term is defined in Minnesota Statutes, section 327C.098. new text end new text begin (b) The investigation shall identify the number of acquisitions that have occurred during the previous ten years and identify the names of the private equity companies and the principals of those companies. new text end new text begin (c) The attorney general, in consultation with the commissioner of health, shall evaluate the impact of the acquired manufactured home parks on: new text end new text begin (1) rents and the cost of services; new text end new text begin (2) the maintenance, physical condition, resiliency, energy efficiency, and other indicators of a sound building infrastructure of the properties acquired or leased by the private equity company; new text end new text begin (3) capital improvements made; and new text end new text begin (4) the financial health of the manufactured home parks after acquisition of ownership or control. new text end new text begin (d) The attorney general shall report the findings of the investigation to the chairs and ranking minority members of the legislative committees with jurisdiction over manufactured home parks by February 15, 2026. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective the day following final enactment. new text end Sec. 3. new text begin APPROPRIATION.new text end new text begin $....... in fiscal year 2026 is appropriated from the general fund to the attorney general to conduct an investigation into the impact on residents and the state of the acquisition of ownership and control of manufactured home parks by private equity companies. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
