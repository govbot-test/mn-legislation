BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. Minnesota Statutes 2024, section 216B.16, is amended by adding a subdivision to read: new text begin Subd. 6e. new text end new text begin Cost recovery for generation and transmission assets. new text end new text begin (a) A public utility that proposes to add an electric generating facility or transmission asset to the public utility's rate base must include in the filing with the commission (1) a quantitative and qualitative assessment of the proposed asset's contribution to the utility's system reliability during times of peak demand, and (2) for an electric generating facility, a calculation of the addition's seasonal effective load carrying capability and the methodology used to make the calculation. new text end new text begin (b) A commission order addressing the addition of an electric generating facility or transmission asset to a public utility's rate base must: new text end new text begin (1) include a finding explaining the extent to which adding the electric generating facility or transmission asset contributes to the utility's system reliability during times of peak demand, as determined by the commission; new text end new text begin (2) determine what, if any, portion of the costs and expenses related to adding the electric generating unit or transmission asset to the public utility's rate base are recoverable costs that may be obtained through rates that are just and reasonable; and new text end new text begin (3) include the following information, as applicable: new text end new text begin (i) the approved increase in recoverable rates; new text end new text begin (ii) the approved rate of return on an electric generating facility or transmission asset, and the duration of the approved return; and new text end new text begin (iii) the amount of the electric generating facility or transmission asset costs and expenses that the commission has determined are not recoverable costs. new text end new text begin (c) For the purposes of this subdivision and subdivision 6f, the following terms have the meanings given: new text end new text begin (1) "electric generating facility" means an asset used to generate or store electricity for retail sale to Minnesota ratepayers; new text end new text begin (2) "recoverable costs" means the revenue a public utility may recover from Minnesota ratepayers to pay for: new text end new text begin (i) the cost of an electric generating facility; new text end new text begin (ii) the cost of a transmission asset; and new text end new text begin (iii) the rate of return a public utility may earn on items (i) and (ii); new text end new text begin (3) "seasonal effective load carrying capability" means the incremental load an electric system is capable of supplying from a particular electric generating facility while maintaining the same level of system reliability during each season, as determined by the Midcontinent Independent System Operator; new text end new text begin (4) "system reliability" means an electric system's ability to deliver electricity to the electric system's customers without interruption or failure; and new text end new text begin (5) "transmission asset" means equipment necessary to transmit electricity from an electric generating facility to a public utility's distribution system for delivery to a retail electric customer. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective the day following final enactment and applies to applicable filings made on or after that date. new text end Sec. 2. Minnesota Statutes 2024, section 216B.16, is amended by adding a subdivision to read: new text begin Subd. 6f. new text end new text begin Reduction in capacity or retirement of generation or transmission assets. new text end new text begin (a) A public utility that proposes to reduce the capacity of or retire from service an electric generating facility or a transmission asset must include in the public utility's filing with the commission evidence that the proposed action does not impair the public utility's ability to fully meet the public utility's system reliability requirements during times of peak demand, including a quantitative and qualitative evaluation of the impact of the proposed action on system reliability. new text end new text begin (b) A commission order addressing the reduction in capacity or retirement of an electric generating facility or transmission facility must contain an explanation describing the extent to which the action impacts the utility's system reliability during times of peak demand, taking into account other scheduled utility asset reductions in capacity or retirements from service. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective the day following final enactment and applies to applicable filings made on or after that date. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
