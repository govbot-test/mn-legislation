Intent. The legislature finds that a combination of state legislative and administrative actions and court decisions have established a public policy of primarily wilderness management for state lands and waters within the Boundary Waters Canoe Areanew text begin Wildernessnew text end . This state policy, together with a similar federal policy and international actions consistent with these state and federal policies, has created an area of hundreds of thousands of acres of land and water containing myriad lakes and streams, wooded shores, virgin forests, and other natural attractions of surpassing scenic beauty and solitude, free from substantially all commercial activities and artificial development such as hydroelectric dams and power lines, resorts, roads, sawmills, and timber harvesting in no-cut zones. The commissioner must assess a civil penalty in the amount of $500,000,000 for violating the terms of the permit established under section 84.523, subdivision 3, paragraph (b), clause (2). new text end new text begin (b) A permittee ordered to suspend operations under paragraph (a) must immediately suspend operations. The permittee may resume operations only after the commissioner has reinstated the permit under paragraph (c). new text end new text begin (c) The commissioner of natural resources may reinstate the permit to mine after the commissioner determines that the permittee has demonstrated that: new text end new text begin (1) the increase in sulfate has been remedied by providing the results of three years of continuous water-quality monitoring conducted by a third party approved by the commissioner of the Pollution Control Agency; and new text end new text begin (2) future mining operations will not increase the level of sulfate in surface water or groundwater by more than zero parts per million. new text end new text begin (d) The permittee must provide all access to the mine that is necessary to conduct water-quality monitoring required under paragraph (c) and cover all costs of the third party conducting the water-quality monitoring. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective the day following final enactment. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
