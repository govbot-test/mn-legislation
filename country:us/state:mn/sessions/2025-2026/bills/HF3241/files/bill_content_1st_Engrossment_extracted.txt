Minnesota Statutes 2024, section 169.471, subdivision 1, is amended to read: Subdivision 1. deleted text begin Television screendeleted text end new text begin Display of electronic contentnew text end in vehicle. deleted text begin No television screen shall be installed or used in any motor vehicle where images from the screen are visible to the driver while operating the motor vehicle except: deleted text end new text begin (a) For purposes of this subdivision: new text end new text begin (1) "dynamic content" means the following types of electronic media or content stored on or displayed by an electronic device: video content, including through video calling, video conferencing, or live-streaming; games; content from a software application that is interactive in nature or involves ongoing and substantial changes to its graphical display; or other content that has the appearance of movement; new text end new text begin (2) "electronic device" includes but is not limited to a wireless communications device, electronic tablet, laptop, digital video player, gaming device, or other portable consumer electronics or wearable device with an electronic display; and new text end new text begin (3) "vehicle interface system" means a component or device for a motor vehicle that: new text end new text begin (i) provides centralized electronic interface to access and control vehicle features, capabilities, and dynamic content, whether or not through digital communication with an electronic device; and new text end new text begin (ii) is affixed to or installed or permanently physically integrated into the vehicle. new text end new text begin (b) When a motor vehicle is in motion or a part of traffic, as provided under section 169.475, subdivision 1, paragraph (d), a person is prohibited from operating the vehicle while: new text end new text begin (1) an electronic device displays dynamic content that is visible to the vehicle operator; or new text end new text begin (2) a vehicle interface system displays dynamic content that is visible to the vehicle operator and is not directly related to vehicle operation, condition, maintenance, safety, or navigation. new text end new text begin (c) The prohibition in paragraph (b) does not apply to: new text end (1) deleted text begin video screensdeleted text end new text begin a device or featurenew text end installed innew text begin anew text end law enforcement deleted text begin vehiclesdeleted text end new text begin vehicle while used in the performance of official dutiesnew text end ; deleted text begin anddeleted text end deleted text begin (3) video screens installed as part of a vehicle control system ordeleted text end new text begin (4) a device or featurenew text end used in intelligent vehicle highway applicationsdeleted text begin .deleted text end new text begin ;new text end new text begin (5) a global positioning system or navigation system that is only capable of being used for navigation purposes; new text end new text begin (6) a wireless communications device that is used in conformance with section 169.475, subdivision 3; and new text end new text begin (7) an electronic device while the device provides a solely passive display. new text end new text begin EFFECTIVE DATE. new text end new text begin This section is effective August 1, 2026, and applies to violations committed on or after that date. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
