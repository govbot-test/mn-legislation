No. 95, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on May 16, 2026 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2honoring Dr. Phua Xiong and the St. Paul Family Medical Center for a distinguished 1.3legacy of health care excellence, pioneering leadership, unwavering commitment 1.4to the advancement of the Hmong people, and transformative impact in shattering 1.5barriers, forever changing the trajectory of Hmong women and girls. 1.6WHEREAS, Dr. Phua Xiong, a pioneer in the field of medicine, immigrated to the United States as 1.7a refugee in 1976 at the age of seven. Her early experiences of life in America inspired her lifelong calling 1.8for health advocacy and social equality; and 1.9WHEREAS, in 1991, Dr. Xiong achieved a historic milestone in medical education as one of the 1.10first Hmong American women to attend the University of Minnesota Medical School. She is among the first 1.11Hmong women to become a physician, not only in America, but in the world; and 1.12WHEREAS, Dr. Xiong has been a tireless advocate for the elevation and advancement of Hmong 1.13girls and women, intentionally working to dismantle the historical and cultural barriers that have long limited 1.14their social status and access to higher education and professional careers; and 1.15WHEREAS, as the founder and medical director of St. Paul Family Medical Center, Dr. Xiong has 1.16dedicated over 25 years to providing full-spectrum family practice care, serving as a beacon of health and 1.17cultural competency in St. Paul; and 1.18WHEREAS, for the past 20 years, the clinic has remained a steadfast pillar of the East Side, offering 1.19essential cross-cultural health care services and breaking down barriers to care for the Hmong community 1.20and diverse populations alike; and 1.21WHEREAS, Dr. Xiong has demonstrated an exceptional commitment to the future of medicine by 1.22mentoring, teaching, and raising up the next generation of health care providers, sharing her clinical expertise 1.23and cultural wisdom to ensure the continued legacy of compassionate health care in America; and 1.24WHEREAS, Dr. Xiong's distinguished career is defined by her deep-rooted commitment to ethics, 1.25her status as a trailblazer for Hmong American women in health care, and her mission to establish a legacy 1.26of love and compassion that extends far beyond her time and the walls of her clinic; and 1.27WHEREAS, Dr. Xiong's commitment to serve is driven by her profound heart of love for the Hmong 1.28people and for those less fortunate, combined with a visionary desire to empower the community to continually 1.29move forward and upward; and 1.30WHEREAS, the community gathers on January 10, 2026, to celebrate these decades of service and 1.31the profound impact Dr. Xiong has made on the health and well-being of the residents of Minnesota; and 1.32NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it extends 1.33its highest commendation and sincere gratitude to Dr. Phua Xiong and the St. Paul Family Medical Center 1.34for their extraordinary contributions to the East Side, the Hmong community, and the state of Minnesota. 1.35BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare an enrolled 1.36copy of this resolution, to be authenticated by the Secretary's signature and that of the Chair of the Senate 1.37Rules and Administration Committee, and transmit it to Dr. Phua Xiong.
