BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA: Section 1. new text begin [115A.1425] MATTRESS RECYCLING GRANTS. new text end new text begin Subdivision 1. new text end new text begin Definitions. new text end new text begin (a) For purposes of this section, the following terms have the meanings given. new text end new text begin (b) "Brand" means a name, symbol, word, or mark that identifies a mattress or box spring, rather than its components, and attributes the mattress or box spring to the owner or licensee of the brand as the producer. new text end new text begin (c) "Producer" means a person that: new text end new text begin (1) has legal ownership of the brand, brand name, or cobrand of a mattress or box spring sold in the state; new text end new text begin (2) imports a mattress or box spring branded by a producer that meets clause (1) when the producer has no physical presence in the United States; new text end new text begin (3) if clauses (1) and (2) do not apply, makes unbranded mattresses or box springs that are sold in the state; or new text end new text begin (4) sells mattresses or box springs at wholesale or retail in the state, does not have legal ownership of the brand, and elects to fulfill the responsibilities of the producer for the mattress or box spring by certifying that election in writing to the commissioner. new text end new text begin (d) "Retailer" means a person who offers mattresses or box springs for sale at retail in the state. new text end new text begin (e) "Sale" or "sell" means transfer of title of a mattress or box spring for consideration, including a remote sale conducted through a sales outlet, catalog, website, or similar electronic means. new text end new text begin Subd. 2. new text end new text begin Recycling assessments. new text end new text begin (a) A producer of a mattress or box spring must add a recycling assessment of $5 to the cost of each mattress and box spring sold to retailers in the state by the producer and must remit the proceeds to the commissioner of the Pollution Control Agency on a quarterly basis. new text end new text begin (b) A retailer must add a recycling assessment of $5 to the purchase price of each mattress and box spring sold by the retailer in the state and must remit the proceeds to the commissioner of the Pollution Control Agency on a quarterly basis. new text end new text begin Subd. 3. new text end new text begin Mattress recycling account. new text end new text begin All proceeds remitted to the commissioner under subdivision 2 must be deposited in the state treasury and credited to a mattress recycling account in the special revenue fund. The amount deposited under this subdivision is annually appropriated to the commissioner to make grants under subdivision 4, and the commissioner may retain up to 2.5 percent of any grant award to pay for the costs of administering the grant. new text end new text begin Subd. 4. new text end new text begin Mattress recycling grants. new text end new text begin The commissioner may use money appropriated from the mattress recycling account to make grants to local governments to expand and support mattress recycling programs in the state. new text end About the Legislature Historical Information Employment/Internships Visiting the Capitol Accessibility Frequently Asked Questions Contact Your Legislator Who Represents Me?
