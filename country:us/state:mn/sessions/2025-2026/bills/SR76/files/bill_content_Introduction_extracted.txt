No. 76, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on April 20, 2026 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2commemorating the 250th anniversary of the founding of the United States 1.3of America. 1.4WHEREAS, July 4, 2026, marks the 250th anniversary, or semiquincentennial, of the 1.5adoption of the Declaration of Independence by the Second Continental Congress in Philadelphia 1.6in 1776, signifying the founding of the United States of America; and 1.7WHEREAS, the Declaration of Independence proclaimed timeless principles of profound 1.8significance to the national heritage of the United States, including that all are created equal and 1.9endowed with certain inalienable rights, including life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness; that 1.10to secure these rights governments are instituted among men; and that governments derive their 1.11just powers from the consent of the governed; and 1.12WHEREAS, the signers of the Declaration of Independence pledged to one another their 1.13lives, their fortunes, and their sacred honor to validate the moral integrity, righteousness, and justness 1.14of the founders' intentions to declare rights to freedom and to independent states, which are necessary 1.15for a free and independent people; and 1.16WHEREAS, a semiquincentennial offers the unique opportunity for the people of Minnesota 1.17to reflect on our history, honor the contributions of all Americans, celebrate our heritage, and look 1.18forward to the future we strive to create for subsequent generations; and 1.19WHEREAS, Minnesota, the 32nd state, has played a significant role through the history of 1.20the United States, contributing to the nation's development, culture, economy, and defense through 1.21the efforts and sacrifices of its citizens; and 1.22WHEREAS, commemorative activities across Minnesota will provide educational 1.23opportunities, foster civic engagement, encourage community participation, strengthen appreciation 1.24for history, and reaffirm our nation's commitment to the ideals of life, liberty, and the pursuit of 1.25happiness; and 1.26WHEREAS, as Benjamin Rush stated in his address to the people of the United States, "The 1.27American war is over: but this is far from being the case with the American revolution."; and 1.28NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Legislature of the State of Minnesota 1.29that it hereby recognizes and commemorates the upcoming 250th anniversary of the founding of 1.30the United States of America and our defining statement of sovereignty, the Declaration of 1.31Independence, on July 4, 2026.
